PREFACE

This is not one of my common practices. I really do not take any special interest in writing letters to people I do not know, some of whom I may never meet before leaving planet earth. However, this particular one has become pertinent in my heart such that I need to pen it down just because of you.

This is not just another book on leadership. It is a compilation of things I have learnt about leadership which I wished I had learnt earlier while on campus.

The purpose of this book is to correct the anomalies in the upcoming leaders and encourage the birth of a new breed of leadership among our youths. It is to bring clarity to the issue of youth leadership, forewarning every leader ahead of time about the challenges of leadership and forearming them with needed facts to do a successful work among the youths.

This purpose can be summarized in two major words:
    (1) To forewarn
    (2) To forearm

Therefore, if you are involved in youth leadership anywhere, this book is meant for you. Regardless of the level at which you exercise your leadership, be it in your church, on your street, in your class, department, faculty, hostel, dormitory, students union government, fellowship, or even in your room! All youth leaders must read this!

As long as you have a measure of influence somewhere, and on someone, no matter how minute or how enormous, and you desire to exercise your influence in a better way, this book is written with you in my mind.

This book will help you navigate through the subtle ocean of youth leadership, charting your course through to success. It is skillfully written to equip every young leader with the necessary tools to press through barriers in order to get the job done.

If this book can change the life of at least one youth leader, I will be the happier for it; knowing that the changed life will affect several other lives, and the several others will, in turn, affect several others, until the leaders of future generations are all touched.

I sincerely, prayerfully, hope that the one life who will start this chain reaction will be you - I mean the person holding this book now.

FOREWORD

Youths are integral, peculiar and important part of the society. They are also bundle of potentials. They have their needs, challenges and aspiration. They are not to be neglected or ignored by any group, community, church or nation but rather harnessed and deployed to fulfill their special place as a segment of the larger population.

To neglect or ignore the youth is to jeopardize and endanger the future and that is why the nation and more so the church should invest in youth ministry. It is also observed that church’s investment in the youth ministry needs to be jerked up as we seek to create an enduring tomorrow that will bring glory to God and blessings to us.

Many churches and organizations are involved in youth ministry but we are not reaping much benefit from it because it is fraught with shortcomings in terms of planning and execution. As a result, many who are involved in youth ministries have either called it quit or are experiencing various forms of frustration. The author, our dear Brother Sam. O. Salau, who is also a youth and has been involved in the youth ministry for so many years, offers insightful ideas on how successful youth ministry in the church could be run.

Every church need to be committed to youth oriented ministry for several reasons: it is a worthy effort and desirable investment. The blessings of a co-ordinated, well planned and God-inspired effort in youth ministry will outlast us. Investing in youth ministry is the wise thing to do, particularly for the church, if we are interested in building a lasting and solid future for the church and the society. Every church, society or nation must get it right on issues concerning the youth and that is why no effort should be spared by all concerned.

One of the reasons ministry among the youth has not been effective as it should be is that some people who are leading the youths today find themselves in the position by accident and are not fully prepared for the challenges it entails. This book provides the guidance on how such people can find their rhythm in the assignment.

As leaders in our respective areas we also have the mandate to influence the youth positively and thus raise them to the right standard ethically, morally and spiritually, and reading this book will help us to appreciate this truth. The issues raised are not only important to running a successful youth ministry but they are equally valuable guides to achieving success in whatever ministry God has entrusted into our care. Paul wrote, “Say to Archippus, that thou fulfill thy ministry” – Colossians 4:17. The book is a motivation for fulfillment in ministry. It is highly commended to youth leaders, those aspiring to be youth leaders, parents, those whose ministry revolves around the youth and all those who desire to see success achieved in youth ministry.

Rev. Felix Meduoye
General Overseer
Foursquare Gospel Church in Nigeria
