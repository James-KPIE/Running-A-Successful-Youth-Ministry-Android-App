Chapter Seventeen

THE NEED FOR A BALANCED YOUTH MINISTRY

This chapter was initially written to be the first chapter of this book. After careful consideration, it was moved from the first to the last, so that it can have a better impact on the readers. Do not make up your mind on what the intention of the author is until you have read the last word in the chapter.

There are so many misconceptions about the youth ministry. So many big ministries and churches do not believe in this arm of the ministry. They may not say this in words, but their actions speak louder than their words.

Most ministries do not believe in their youths enough to give them free hands to operate in the church. They tend to cage them, believing they are not mature enough to handle certain

spiritual things. They are treated as babes in the Lord, who can only be spoon-fed with the milk of the word.

Unknown to them, most of these youths they still refer to as spiritual babies, have handled serious leadership positions at various levels in school, both primary and tertiary, and have been able to deliver results that are astounding, to the progress of their school fellowships and the work of God on their campuses.

Some other ministries who pride themselves in the freedom they have given to their youths only run this arm as a Single's Ministry. All they ever discuss in the weekly youth meetings are issues about sex and relationships, as though that is the only important area of their lives. Little or nothing is said about personal development, about career building, about financial security, and about building a legacy after one is long gone.

Other ministries run theirs like a social gathering. The youths only meet on special days: say public holidays, so as to get familiar with one another. They go on excursions to the beach and other places of interests, host love feasts, and organize praise nights so as to form a bond of unity amidst themselves.

There are still some who are very religious about it. They run the youth group as an arm of the church that meets either monthly or bimonthly and the order of program for these services have nothing youth-like or youth-oriented in them. All they do is to indoctrinate the youths with beliefs and traditions of their denominations, and they expect the youths to swallow them all, hook, line and sinker, without a question. Any youth that raises a question is termed a rebel and is blackmailed out-

rightly. Some of these beliefs have been in existence before the advent of biro and paper, and are seen by the youths as out-dated, especially when there is no candid scriptural evidence to back them up.

All of these four approaches of running the youth arm of the church that are mentioned above are not balanced enough for a youth church of the 21st century.

For the purpose of this study, The Youth Ministry is defined as an arm of the larger ministry or church that is specially designed to meet the basic fundamental needs of the youths in the ministry.

One can safely conclude from the definition above that the major essence of a youth arm of the ministry is to meet the special needs that are unique and peculiar to the youths, especially in that ministry. The main challenge with the church of today is that they want to use the youths in the church, whose peculiar needs have neither been addressed nor met, to meet the needs of the other youths outside the church.

This is where the problem of the 21st century church is. There doesn’t seem to be much difference between the youth who are expected to be used by God to bring in the other youths into the church and the youths they expect them to bring in. The church has put the cart before the horse. Until the needs of the youths in the church are met, it is going to be a herculean task trying to use them to bring in other youths to the fold whose needs also need to be met.

One can safely conclude that the church today does not understand the place of the youths in the end time harvest that the Lord has promised his church. If she does, she will appreciate the importance of these youths enough to meet their needs, and these youths whose needs have been met will in turn bring in more youths whose needs need to be met, without being forced to.

IMPORTANCE OF A YOUTH MINISTRY

Shortly before we consider the needs of today's youths, let us examine the importance of a youth ministry. It is a popular knowledge that when the purpose of a thing is not known, the abuse of it is grossly inevitable. The reason why most pastors and churches abuse the presence of the youths in their churches is because their eyes have not yet been opened to the purpose of this unique group.

Discussed below are reasons why every church should run a youth arm, and do it right.

1. FOR CONTINUITY

One major reason every church organization should be encouraged to start a youth arm is for the continuity of the church doctrines. The uniqueness and beliefs of each denomination is better passed down to the youths who will still stay around for a longer time than the adults who are already on their way out of the dance floor of life.

Most churches do not include these youths in the vital decision making process of the church, either as contributors or spectators. All they do is just tell the youths of the decisions they have arrived at. The youths that are expected to defend the

course of the church tomorrow, long after the adults are gone, do not even know the reason for the course, talk less of believe in such course. This is why some good churches do not stay around for too long. They wither away after the first few generations.

It is commonplace to see that these youths only manage to cope in these churches basically because it is their parent’s church, and they have no choice. As long as they are under the roof of their parents, they have to attend these churches. This is forced submission.

Such youths, after gaining admission to the university, with a little air of freedom, change their church immediately or at worst, wait until they graduate, or marry, and then relocate to where they can choose by themselves what church meets their needs.

This seems to be the reason why the youth population in the new generation churches seems to be growing in leaps and bounds, and it seems the traditional church is only trying to catch up with them. The new generation churches are readily meeting the needs of the youths while the traditional churches feel the youths are just a bunch of Sunday Morning Bench Warmers whose attendance is only needed to increase the population of the church.

Every church that desires to cross boundaries and impact more than one generation must have a representation of the youths in each of the arms of the ministry, including the highest decision making council. It is not so much that the youths can show off at these meetings, but for continuity sake.

2. TO AVOID CLASHES

There is always a cold war, a constant silent battle, between the old school and the new school. This is caused by the differences in the belief system of those who still hold rigidly to traditional way of doing things and that of the new generation believers who, though appreciate the scriptures, however feel that the old school have read their personal meanings to what some part of the scriptures say, and as such are misinterpreting scriptures.

Some of the points of clashes between the two schools are things like the dress code, dance steps, hair styles, adornments with jewelries and the like.

Most of these things that the outgoing generation frowns at are seen by the incoming generation as the order of the day. Hence, the adults feel the youths are carnal minded and not committed spiritually to the things of God, while the youths feel that the adults are overzealous, and that this zeal without balanced knowledge makes them look too spiritual for their own kind of belief.

This age long battle has made many leading churches in times past lose their relevance among the youths and these churches are on the brink of going into extinction today.

The main reason the adults give to support this action that the youths will rather see as extra biblical ordinances is that most of these things which the youths want to do are strange to our culture. They are quick to forget that, although the elders grew up under a particular culture and so can keep defending traditions and cultural practices on which the Bible does not

have a strong stand, the youths of today grew up under a mixed culture in a global village and it may not be easy to force them to conform into the demands of a particular cultural setting they were not used to.

Although the youths may have their own excesses which the outgoing generation must not fail to correct, the adults must learn to stop exactly where the scriptures stopped, and be silent where the scripture is silent, so that they will not be seen as unconsciously defending the cultures and the traditions of men in the name of defending the scriptures.

3. TO HAVE LIBERTY OF WORSHIP

Youths like to do things their own way, as long as it is not against the written Word of God. They have their own style of speech that they all understand but may be strange to the adults. They have their dance steps, hip hop styled praise songs, loud eardrum bursting worship songs, and the like.

Their idea of what a worship service should look like is entirely different from that of the older generation. While one believes that the sanctuary should have some sanctity with as little noise and distractions as possible, the other believes that the difference between a mortuary and a sanctuary is the noise. So, organized distractions are allowed.

These, most times, do not go down well with the older generations who may see them as going too far and taking God for granted. With this type of mind-set, it becomes almost impossible for one group to, wholeheartedly, worship in the presence of the other without being aggrieved or offended. This

difference in opinion is caused by the generation gap between the two parties.

This realization which the church has failed to come to terms with has been identified long ago by experts in the marriage counseling programs. They discovered that when a man marries a woman who is 10-15years younger than he is, they are likely to have some issues at the family front which is not common to those whose age differences are not that far apart. This is because they are not of the same generation, and the wider the generation gap, the more the issues they have to contend with.

This is the same issues we have in church today. We expect that people of diverse generations will worship together under the same atmosphere peacefully. The father is 60years of age, the mother is 55years, the firstborn is 30years and the last born is 15years. They all have different views about life, and we will expect all of them to worship together under the same roof for years without any issues? We will expect that the preacher will preach one single message and still meet the needs of all of them, and do that for 52 Sundays in a year!

The way out before now is to suppress the youths and force them to endure the adults’ way of worship all through the year, or better still, give them one Sunday in the year, called their youth anniversary, or youth week, to have their way. In most churches, from experience, there is always a problem after such anniversary because one adult got offended at one youth’s behavioral attitude in church that day.

This, most times, is always the issue. The older people complain that the younger people’s style and mode of worship is

becoming a stumbling block to numerous other elders who want to worship God but will not think of it because of the way the youths worship. They always forget that the reverse of that statement is also true. Except for those whose parents attend some of these traditional churches, so many youths also will not think of going to church if this is the only church around where they live. The elders are also unconsciously, in the name of defending the faith, becoming a stumbling block to the youths.

The final solution now is for the youths to live a hypocritical life, since there is no freedom of worship. They stay in the church, but tune their receiving antennal off. This is why you notice that, if they find themselves in a large meeting, youths hardly sit in one place. They move about in large numbers, chatting and playing. If it is a small meeting where they can be easily noticed by their parents, rather than move around, what they do is bring out their gadgets, cell phones or whatever, and then connect to a larger audience outside the meeting place through a social media site.

As much as this attitude is not to be encouraged, they do this simply to while away time, since they cannot flow with whatever worship style they have found themselves in. Those who do not want to live this hypocritical life are likely to withdraw from the church and find a more comfortable church around if their parents agree with them.

This is why every ministry needs to start floating a youth arm: to give the youths liberty of worship.

4. TO CREATE A SENSE OF BELONGING

Everyone appreciates something he can call his own, rather than manage that of another. A youth arm of the church will give the youths a sense of belonging. It will not be their parent's church. It will be their church.

Once you give the youths this type of opportunities, they will do all in their power to see the church grow. Most times, such churches have grown much more than their mother churches in numerical strength. The youths who have not been too involved before in the main church will be more committed now, having found a place of expression for his inner gifts. Their workforce will increase and their evangelism will bring great growth.

5. A PLACE OF REFUGE

Many youths are out there today who though have lost it, but have realized their present sinful state, and are willing to give Christ a trial. These youths have indulged in so many unthinkable things in their days of ignorance, but have now realized their need for the Savior and are ready to yield to Him.

However, most times, the physical look of these youths does not really represent the progressive transformation that is on-going in their souls. They still look like who they really are - a sinner that needs the touch of the Savior to become a saint.

If these ones mistakenly get into a church designed traditionally for the elders, they feel so out of place and uncomfortable and may likely withdraw back to the world. To make matters worse, some church will not even allow such to come into their place of worship, talk less of giving them an opportunity to meet with the Savior who can fully transform both their soul and their body.

These churches need to be reminded that, in case they have forgotten, the church may today look like an association of saints on their way to heaven, but it is primarily a group of sinners on their way to hell, blood washed by the lamb and turned saints so they can turn other sinners to saints.

Such youths as described above may find a youth church to be a place of refuge where they can hide their heads pending the time when the Lord will perfect the on-going transformation, both in their spiritual lives and their physical look.

CREATING THE NEEDED BALANCE

The main targets of the devil’s kingdom in these last days are not the elders; he is already giving up on them. His warfare is targeted more at the future generations. That is why all of the cultural wars in churches are primarily to discourage the youths from giving their best of service to the Lord. Satan knows that once you can capture the leaders of tomorrow today, it becomes easy for him to control the followers of tomorrow at will, and as such, it becomes increasingly easy to silence the church of tomorrow when the time comes.

With this in mind, the elders of the church should do their best to encourage the youths to offer their heartfelt service to God, and not place much more burdens on them than the scriptures has placed. The elders need to stand for the truth by earnestly fighting for the truth, both in their generation and in the incoming generation. They need to guide the youths on how to walk before God perfectly and how not to fulfill the desires of the flesh.

The leaders of today need to teach the leaders of tomorrow why they cannot always have their ways when it comes to spiritual matters. However, in doing this, they must support their arguments and claims from the standpoint of the scriptures, and not just from personal experiences or biased mindsets. The youths will believe more in the testimony of a rightly divided word of truth than in the words of the doctrinal book of their denomination.

The outgoing generation should spiritually equip the youths to prepare them adequately for the real war the devil is waging against the existence of the church and not allow generational differences, traditional mindsets and cultural wars to put a division between them and the great army the Lord is building to fight His course in these last days.

On the other hand, the leaders of tomorrow also need to know that one cannot serve God and mammon together. This generation needs to be very careful because the line between worldliness and godliness in getting thinner by the day, and if one is not careful in this age and time, one can keep dribbling here and there around the touch line, still thinking one is still within the safe haven, without knowing he has crossed over the touchline. This may be catastrophic. This is why the Corinthians were admonished: whoever thinks he is still in good standing should always be careful to examine himself from time to time, lest he falls before he realizes.

This generation needs a teachable spirit, humble heart and obedience to always listen when the elders talk. They need to know that their desires to embrace, exhibit, and adopt the end time mode of dressing, dancing, talking, thinking, acting and

reacting while yearning for freedom to ‘serve and worship’ the Holy God, if care is not taken, may cause them to begin to take actions that contradict the postulations of the scriptures.

If we need to have a balanced church, these two seemingly warring factions must learn to see things from the eyes of the scriptures, unpolluted by either the old school reasoning that still holds onto what was obtainable in the past and will not want to see what God is doing today, or by the new school mentality that wants to sacrifice godliness and contentment for an opportunity to look more like the world on daily basis. The truth must be told: the more of the world they resemble, the less of Christ they manifest.
