Chapter Fifteen

CREATING BALANCES

Your life needs your attention; give your life the attention it needs. Do not kill yourself for leadership sake. If you die, the best the youths will do is to name a memorial after you, and then you are forgotten in no time. Someone else will replace you and the job continues. So, for the sake of other followers who will still need a good leader like you in the nearest future, take care of yourself!

You need to live a balanced life. Balance in life is needed for successful leadership. This is the age of extremes, but you will need to learn how to maintain balances. The other aspects of your life should not just suffer because you are a leader. You will need to learn how to say no to some things. Most leaders do not know that each time they say ‘yes’ to anything that demands some part of their time, they need to say ‘no’ to some other things to which they previously said ‘yes’, so as to create a balance in their schedule. You may think that you have so much free time, but you do not have more than the average 24hours everyone has.

The ability to learn to say ‘no’ without feeling guilty is one virtue all leaders should have. If you keep saying ‘yes’ without learning how to say ‘no’, before you know it, you will overcrowd your life with activities without any free margin for yourself, and very soon, you will fizzle away.

If you always feel fagged out or tired, it is time to take a serious look at your life and discover how to bring it back into balance. You may need to delegate some activities (flip back to the chapter on Delegating Responsibilities and read it again), say ‘no’ to some, and reduce the time allocated to others. Whatever you do, create space for yourself by creating some balance in your life. If you do not, other areas of your life, (for example, your family, career, academic, relationship with God) will suffer.

There are five major areas of your life which you need to give serious attention to. Do all in your power to strike a balance between them all. All these five put together will enable you to create the necessary balance between your life and the demands of the leadership position.

1. PROPER REST

It will come to a time when, considering the weight of things yet undone, you will tend to become over-busy. Tremendous pressure from all angles which desire your personal attention may want to tear you apart. Irrespective of these pressures, take time out to rest. You will discover at the end of the day that you are more fruitful after a time of rest, than any other time.

Youth leadership can be draining, especially with all of the pressures from all sides. Some of these include your education, your spiritual life, your social life, your relationships with loved ones, your personal daily routines, coupled with the leadership responsibility and the general expectations of your followers from you. Each day is exhaustive with its myriad of demands from all around you, and each of these demands striving to extract a portion of you.

The demands of your office require a great deal of time, energy and commitment. You will need rest to reinvigorate. If you do not give yourself enough time to rest, you are abusing your body.

Many people do not have the physical strength to discipline themselves so as to create a time for their bodies to rest. This is not ideal. You are not a robot. You are human. Make rest and relaxation a priority.

Learn to do what you must do today and you will be able to do what you want to do for long. If you deny yourself of the natural refreshment that only sleep can bring, you will leave before your time, and someone else who may not be as good as you are will carry on with your assignment

Take siestas. You need to. Give yourself time for good sleep. You do not have to sleep ten hours per night if you do not have to. Whatever time you devote to sleep per night, sleep qualitatively. Take all worries away from your mind when it is time to sleep; worry kills. The little time you have to sleep, sleep like a baby. It will help you to achieve more when you are awake.

Everyone needs good sleep. To oscillate between all of these responsibilities without adequate rest periods can lead to leadership imbalance. You will become emotionally unstable and physically spent.

There must be a work-leisure balance. Being busy is not synonymous with being fruitful. Being active does not really

mean you are productive. Set your priorities right. You must be disciplined enough to say no to unproductive activities. This will need some courage as you must be prepared to disappoint some people.

Leadership is not just about sitting in the office, having hours of unending meetings and processing papers. It is about making things happen, especially the more important and more significant things. To make these things happen you need a cool brain, a relaxed mind and renewed energy.

The period of rest is the period for renewal of energy and strength. You will be more useful to the generation you are serving and the one to come if you take time out to rest than if you do not. He who rests, lasts. If you do not take time out of your busy schedule to rest today, you will be forced to rest someday, when sickness knocks you down.

Do not let your busy schedule in the office cause you to forget your sleep or neglect your bed. You are mortal, and there is a limit to what you can do. This is not to encourage slothfulness in leadership. Only magicians with modernized magic wand can do the impossible in this jet age. However, anyone, yourself inclusive, can make possible the impossible by simply giving it your best. Give it your best shot, but find time to rest.

You will not impress anyone by simply being a ‘workaholic’ leader. They hardly notice your sleeplessness, and if they do, they do not appreciate it as long as it does not bring commensurate progress to the organization. Worse still, it really does not make the job any easier, neither does it make your leadership more acceptable.

2. PROPER EXERCISE

Once-a-week exercise will help you to alleviate the stress accumulated over the week. This weekly exercise, to a great extent, will help you control your stress level. When the stress level is unchecked, your health is threatened and your effectiveness, as a leader, is challenged.

Find a hobby; it may stretch your nerves. Balance your intensive workload with leisure activity. The more responsibility leaders get, the more they neglect the care of their body. Take good care of your body and you will be too grateful when it begins to reward you. It will have a positive effect on your soul and spirit.

If you can afford it, register in a gym or get a physical health instructor who will attend to you at home, weekly. If you cannot afford any of that, engage yourself in the normal Saturday morning workouts. An early morning jug, a number of press-ups, sit-ups, some minutes on the mill or with the skipping rope and the like, will save your body from deterioration and put you in good shape.

3. PROPER STUDY

If you are not a student, this may not affect you seriously.

Student leadership is time consuming and energy sapping; do not expect to have an easy time at it. Most students do not know this until they get involved in it. It is always much more demanding than they expect.

This is why many students who have the opportunity to lead other students always have this problem. They do not have as much time as they should have for their academics.

You will hardly have time to study, but it is important to make out time to study. Your books need your attention. Find the time to attend to it by all means. You are a student. Never stop being a student.

Many students may see your position as one of privilege and honor, secretly desiring the post, but only the head that wears the crown knows that “uneasy lies the head that wears the crown.”

For more on how to excel as a student, you should get the author’s books titled “Secrets Of Successful Students” and “How To Boost Your GP”.

4. PROPER DIET

Take care of yourself. Eat well. You cannot just be expending energy without replenishing it or else you will soon be depleted. In order to replace the energy dissipated in carrying out your official functions as a leader, you will need a very balanced diet.

You may not have the time to cook; you rarely will anyway, but you do not really have to. There are good restaurants all around nowadays. Once in a while, give yourself a good treat at any of the big eateries around. It will do your system a whole lot of good.

Do not subject yourself to one particular type of food every day. The temptation to do this is so high that so many leaders do not

know when they fall for it, either because the food is the most readily available or affordable. If you do this, you will deny your body system the nutrients it needs to function properly. It may not break down the same month you start doing it, but the day it decides to, you will discover that there is nothing as good as eating good food and being in good health, and that the pain to get a good plate of food thrice a day is preferable to the pain of being injected with syringes and drips.

Be careful what you eat, discipline your body. Do not just take junks. Balance your diet. Ordinary primary school health science teaches clearly that there are six classes of food: protein, vitamin, carbohydrate, fats and oil, mineral salt and water. Make sure they are all represented in your food daily.

5. PROPER COMPANION

You will need to create time for your intimate relationships. It may look as though the only important people in your life now are your followers. Remember that some people were in your life before you became a leader, and if you do not die in the leadership position, you will still need them back in your life. Do not allow the demands of your leadership position make you deny them of your companionship when they should have it.

If you are married, create time for your wife and children. Your wife may understand your busy schedule, but your children will be too young to understand. They have no other father to act in your stead, so you must be ready to attend to them whenever they need the attention of their father.

If you are not married, give attention to your partner. Do not let her back off the relationship on the account of your lack of

attention for her. Also, create time for your immediate family: your father, mother, sisters and your brothers. They probably have not served as a youth leader before and so will not appreciate that the reason why they cannot have your attention is because you have some new people in your life who have stolen your heart away from your family.

PLAN YOUR TIME

To get all of these things done, you will need to be fully in charge of your time. Youth leadership requires a lot of time, energy and physical resilience. It will definitely impact your health, relationships, academics & ambition either positively or negatively, all depending on the way you handle it.

No matter how busy you are, find time for yourself. Even the president of the greatest nation on earth has time for himself. Plan your time so that you will have time for all you have to do. Being busy is not the same as working. Filling time is not the same thing as time management. Being a workaholic is not an efficient way of using time. It is very easy to get too busy to be effective! You may not believe this until you get there.

Guard your time jealously. Do not let irrelevant, unscheduled, appointments encroach into your time unnecessarily. What is urgent may not be important and what is important may not be urgent. When you place each where it belongs, you will have more time for your life. Begin to value your time so much that you want to keep some for yourself. Until you value your time this much, you will not do anything with it for yourself. You will only work for the progress of other people.

AN IDEAL DAY FOR A YOUTH LEADER

1. Start your day with prayers. You will achieve more by starting up with God.

2. Write out a list of things you want to accomplish in the course of the day. This list is called “to-do-list”.

3. Rewrite them in order of importance or according to their scale of preference.

4. If you are a student, separate at least an hour to go through your school notes, review past lectures, and finish up your practical and assignments. This brief revision early in the morning will help you a lot since you may be very busy during the day, and your concentration level will not be as high as in the mornings.

5. If you are not a student, use this time to read a personal developmental book so as to add some value to your life, on daily basis.

6. Make calls or send text to everyone who in one way or the other will contribute to the success of your day. This text or call will remind them of your expectations from them in case they have forgotten. These will include

(i) Team members who have been assigned some responsibilities to be carried out on that day.

(ii) The people you intend to meet with during the course of the day.

(iii) Other leaders you may need to get something from during the course of the day.

(iv) Any other appointments you may not want to miss.

7. Do your toiletries and your household chores (bathing, washing, ironing, cleaning, eating)

8. Go to your office to attend to your office work. (Go for lectures if you are a student)

9. Set out for appointments that fall into the office/school period, with proper permission from your boss in the office or the lecturers, but try to avoid this as much as possible. Every appointment that you must attend to during the day should be scheduled at your free period in school or lunch time in the office.

10. Eat and rest after office/school hours.

11. Attend to other appointments till evening.

12. Take timeout to read something, either meaningful or just entertaining. You may also play some games or watch a movie. Just find a way to relax.

13. Have your dinner.

14. Go to bed.
