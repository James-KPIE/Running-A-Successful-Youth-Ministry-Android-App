Chapter Six

BUILD A SOUND WORKFORCE

Your workforce, literally means the human force you work with. They are the workers available for any or all of your operations.

From the definition above, your workforce will include those who work hand in hand with you. They serve as your key leaders, youth executives, managerial team, planning committee or implementation board. These are people in whose hands the success of your leadership directly depends. They run your programs, oversee your projects, and sit with you to take crucial decisions on all relevant matters. These are the ones to whom you designate some vital aspects of your responsibility and authority. They are those who make things happen for you. Without them, there is a limit to the success you can make, irrespective of your plans and your efficiency.

Many good people with good intentions have gotten to leadership positions and fumbled simply because they did not have the right set of people to work with. This is because leadership is not a one man show; it is a collective drive. If you have this right perspective of what a workforce is, it will help you choose the right personnel for the specific tasks at hand.

Nothing builds up a winning team like putting the right people in the right place. You do not have to put only your choice friends or closest colleagues in positions that matter. It may generate a negative reaction from other youths. Apart from the crisis it will cause within the group, you will not also enjoy the best service from them, since they are not rightly qualified for the position they occupy. When it comes to choice of leaders, team members or executives, you should be more interested in functions and abilities, than relationships and friendships.

Most times when youths meet to take serious decisions, they spend hours on end without reaching a conclusive end, and most times have to postpone the meeting to a later time. This is because the configuration of the people at the meeting differs; hence their differing opinions. If there is a common goal, with a common focus, and a common drive, meetings need not take eternity before decisions are made.

Your choice of co-leaders should be youths who share common core values with you. They should be limited to people you can relate with in unity. If you do otherwise, your executive meetings will be filled with hot arguments and serious debates. You will always find it difficult to reach an agreement.

Since you do not share the same views, it will be pretty hard to come to a consensus on issues, points of value will always differ, interests will differ, and pursuits will differ. Every issue discussed will be seen like the case of the half-filled cup. Some will want to jump at the offer of the half-filled cup while the other party will detest the opinion of considering the acceptance of the half empty cup. One side sees the filled half; while the other side sees the empty half.
Life is like this! While some people praise God for roses in the midst of their thorns, others complain bitterly of thorns in the midst of their roses. Perceptions differ! You will save yourself a whole lot of stress if you choose people with whom you see eye-to-eye on, if not all issues, at least, majority of them. Select people with common goals, principles, ideas, and purposes so as to have a cooperate effort.

The projects you want to undertake will be one of the major deciding factors to consider in choosing your workforce. This is because projects succeed or fail because of the people involved.

After highlighting what you want to achieve in your tenure in office, ask yourself: “Who do I need to make this happen?” It is important to have an accurate and detailed answer to this simple question above as it will aid you guard against a lot of errors which many other leaders before you have made.

You will need to choose people who have gifts and talents which are relevant to your projects so that their gifts can be useful for the progress of the group. Answer this question for each step you have highlighted. If you can find the correct answer to this question for each step of the way, your job becomes simplified and things naturally begin to happen around you.

The ‘whom’ you do ‘what’ with is as important as the ‘when’. The ‘what’ and the ‘when’ may be right, but until the ‘whom’ is right, progress will not be recorded. Great leader always know when to do what and with whom. Formally or informally, screen eligible youths for each position and see to it that only those who are qualified enough get to the place of authority as co-leaders with you. This is very important because the choice of your co-leaders will determine, to a large extent, your success in the office.

Spend time with your workforce. It takes playing together, chatting together, arguing together and robbing minds together to learn how to think together, and with time, reason alike, for the good of the organization.

Let your battery charge the battery of other people. Make a deposit into their lives. There is no success without a successor. These are the ones who will carry on your dream long after you are gone. If it happens that the leadership baton changes hands and you are no longer opportune to lead them, it is very possible that the leadership baton will be handed over to one of those whom you have raised. These ones will see to the successful completion of the projects which you initiated but unable to complete. This will make your leadership impact transcend generations to come.

If they are not in the new leadership, but are still in the youth group, the new leadership will find it difficult to stop them from continuing the projects you started as long as they are relevant. You raised them, and they owe you the privilege to see your dream come true.

Plant seeds in everyone; someday they will germinate, and you will reap from them. Teach them all you wished your predecessor had taught you while in office. Great success without a good successor is a great failure. It is these ones you groomed who will carry on your legacy at the end of your term in office. If you teach them well, you will have no fear if one of them finally succeeds you when your tenure is over. You will be relaxed; rest assured that the projects you started are in safe hands.

It is possible to discover some people who know much more than you in certain areas. This is one of the ironies of life. You do not have to compete with them. Do not envy them, rather, employ them. Enlist them in your workforce, and silently learn from them until you have known what they know (that is if you think you will need the knowledge later on in life).

Do not be threatened by their knowledge. Welcome varieties of gifts and talents. Intentionally look out for and engage people who are smarter than you are, or who have capacities to become better than you, if well trained, and invest in. If you can invest in their lives, their personal development will propel the development of the organization.

UNDERSTANDING THE STAGES OF YOUTH LEADERSHIP

During your tenure as a leader, no matter how long or how short, no matter how committed you are to serving your followers, you will experience three different stages.

Every leader, good or bad, has passed through these stages in the past and none has been able to stay on just one of the three, permanently. You also will continually move from one of these stages to another. Your efficiency per time will depend on how you react during each of these stages.

These three stages of leadership are not peculiar to leaders leading the youths alone. They are common to all leaders. So, do not begin to think that running away from the youth ministry will save you from them. Knowing how to handle well each stage will prepare you for the future challenges of leadership at a higher cadre when the time comes.

1. THE ENTHUSIASTIC STAGE
This is the stage when so much progress is made in so little a time. Your enthusiasm is at its peak. Your follower’s excitement is unequalled. Their zeal is plentiful and the energy is robust. Everything you want done is carried out to specifications without any delay and with little or no opposition.

This is the most enjoyable period for any leader. Everyone around him cooperates for his success. Things fall in their rightful places of their own accord. Opportunities to make very visible impact on his followers open up by themselves and one always leads to the other in quick successions. It is a period of heaven on earth for every leader. He is liked by his followers, supported by his work force, appreciated by other leaders, celebrated by his colleagues, and encouraged by higher authorities.

Any leader who starts at this stage will feel that youth leadership is as easy as drinking a bottle of soft drink with plastic straw. If this is what leadership is all about, no leader will have problems with his followers. This is just one of the three stages of leadership.

This is the best time for any leader to make necessary impact on his organization. Every little effort attracts great results. During this stage, each of his impacts is amplified by supernatural forces to produce unending results. Wise leaders maximize this period by using it to pursue their best projects.

The unpalatable news is that this stage, as much as every leader will desire it to, is not always permanent; it fades out sooner than people expect. So, if you find yourself in this stage in your leadership quest, make the best of it. Do all you have to now that the people are willing to yield to each of your impulses. They are ready to follow if only you are ready to lead aright.

Give your best projects supreme priority during this period. Whatever you cannot achieve during this stage, you probably may not be able to achieve at any other phase.

2. THE ROUTINE STAGE
This is the stage where not too much progress is made, although everything is functioning smoothly. Things go as planned, but not too much improvement is recorded. It is more of a constant flat terrain experience unlike the enthusiastic stage where skyrocketing achievements are the order of the day.

Of a fact, things are not really as easy at this stage, but it is not also that difficult. It is just that the people are not as cooperative as they used to be. They give so many excuses for not doing what is expected of them and they are not very excited about you and your dream. The excuses they give though will be justifiable. They are busy with office work, they have pressure at the home front, they need time to settle some pressing needs, they are preparing for an exam, and others too numerous to highlight here.

They still like you, at least they try to wear a smile each time they see you from afar, and they still give you a warm embrace each time they have the opportunity to get closer, but they now have more precious things to do with their time than running helter-skelter with another man’s dream.

At first, you may begin to insinuate that it is because they heard some bad or untrue news about you, or that your enemies have won them over. After a careful research, you will discover that no one is really at the root of this. They are just being human.

Deciding to confront them is encouraged but you must know that it may not yield any change because they all have reasonable and tenable excuses to give for not rallying round you or running with your projects as they used to. Whatever excuse they give, you will be handicapped.

With wisdom, every leader can still successfully cope during this stage. Since you still have their allegiance and support, you have no problem. What they have only denied you is their 100% commitment, like before. So, it really is no serious problem.

All you need to do is to accept the little commitment they have to offer at this stage and keep encouraging them to do more. It is not the time to chastise them or send nasty messages to them. It is not the time to “let them know the stuff you are made of”, neither is it the time to “show them your other side”. It simply is the time to cope with the best they are willing to offer and allow them attend to whatever it is they need to attend to. Life is not a bed of roses. The tide will soon change, and tomorrow, you may be back at the enthusiastic phase.

Leaders whose first experience is the enthusiastic stage may begin to think that the change in the attitude of the followers is as a result of something they used to do which they no longer do. If you discover that there were things you stopped, you are encouraged to retrace your steps. However, do not be too disappointed if this does not bring the result you expect. Their reaction to you is not because of what you stopped, it is because no condition is permanent in life.

Leaders whose first experience is this stage may have to pray for grace to cope.

3. THE FRUSTRATION STAGE
This is the most difficult and the only almost unbearable period of leadership. It is a stage most leaders do not pray to experience. It is characterized by frustrations and turmoil. This is the time you attract the greatest degree of stresses and strains. You come face to face with the very low experiences of leadership.

At this stage, the level of commitment from your people begins to dwindle drastically and their cooperation whittle fast away with time. Those who were very supportive before will turn their backs on you, or at worst, keep away from you completely. They will not want to have anything to do with you anymore, and they will not hide it. They will not pick your calls, they will turn down your invitations, avoid you as much as possible, and shun everything that has to do with you. It is even possible that you will begin to get criticisms from those who were loyal to you in the past.

During this stage, your influence on the group will gradually become limited by the day, and some people may secretly begin to gang up against your leadership. Those who know your secrets, and who used to serve as your backbones may even turn against you and worse still, teach others to do the same. It is a period of betrayals. The progress of projects slows down almost to a crawl and things will no longer as planned. It is the direct opposite of the enthusiastic stage.

At this stage, the leader will constantly face pressure from almost all angles. It will look as though he was not the same leader who was being celebrated a while ago. The same things he did which earned Him accolades a while ago, now begin to attract condemnation. Decisions that seemed so right before, now seem so wrong. Followers begin to lose confidence in him. They no longer will appreciate his noticeable efforts and commendable dedication.

It is not uncommon for procrastination to seep in at this moment. Things which you did with utmost urgency and all enthusiasm now become things you put off indefinitely. Your productivity and your efficiency rate take a decline on the graph of your performance. There is no longer any source of motivation
This is a very discouraging stage for any leader. At this phase, it is likely that depression will set in for an inexperienced leader. The leader soon begins to lose his fire and passion. The enthusiasm, attraction and the thrill are gone. Doubts, fear, rejection, insecurity, and a host of other negative emotions flood in. He loses focus and his faith is about to waver. It is a period of exhaustion, confusion and discouragement. He sits down and watches the organization he built over the years crumble before his very eyes. It is a bitter experience for leaders. During this stage, leaders begin to have a miserable feeling which often leads to self-pity. Far too often, they begin to question their own ability, capability, experience and professionalism.

If this stage is not well handled, self-depreciation begins to set in. This is dangerous as it can degenerate into low self-esteem and inferiority complex, both of which can hinder their effectiveness. If you are not truly a man, you will, almost, be forced to the resignation phase. You will begin to nurse negative feelings about yourself. Thoughts like; “maybe I really should not be the leader here”, “maybe I am not capable enough”, or “maybe I do not have what it takes” will begin to flood your mind. At this stage, the urge to quit will be the dominating thought of weak-willed leaders. However, this stage, like other stages, is not permanent. It is a phase that will soon fade away, bringing in again the enthusiastic stage.

So many things may be responsible for this period, the chief of which is a wrong decision by the leader. Youths are predictable. One wrong decision can turn them all against you, just as one right decision can make all of them flock around you like chicken round mother hen. This is why you need to be careful about the decisions you make and the advice you take. If you refuse to take just one right step at the right time, you may pay with a thousand steps later in life

Once you find yourself at this stage, the first question to ask yourself is “Where did I go wrong? The answer to this question will not be farfetched. If you carefully examine your immediate actions in recent times, just at the beginning of this stage of your leadership, you will be able to trace the source of the challenge you are facing.

If you are unable to place your hands on any, you can talk to those you trust among your followers; enquire from them the reason for the change in their attitude to you. No matter how corrupt their minds are, at least one of them will be willing to share with you what the real problem is. This is why it is advisable to call them individually and not address them as a group. That way, you will find out more truths than you will in a public meeting. You may even be able to handle some of the issues raised privately at individual levels rather than allow someone expose the skeleton in your cupboard before the general assembly.

Once you discover what the issue is, then your problem is over. Take the necessary step to put things straight. If you need to apologize, please tender a sincere apology. If you need to loosen your grip on an opinion, please relax your nerves. If you need to revert on a decision, be quick to press the reverse gear. This will bring you back to the enthusiastic stage faster than you think. You will gain back the allegiance and the support of your followers one more time, and you will be glad you did.

Irrespective of the precautions you take, this stage will still come. The precautions cannot stop it from materializing; it can only reduce its duration and minimize its effect on you and the group at large.
