Chapter Seven

COMMUNICATE YOUR VISION CLEARLY

So much depends on how you communicate your vision to your followers. In communicating your vision to them, you must speak the language they can understand. You must know what to communicate and how to communicate it. The extent to which your followers understand what you communicate will be the extent of their support for you.

The difference between peaceful audience and a rioting crowd is in what is communicated to them and the difference between obedient followers and rebellious mammoth is in the way the information is communicated. No matter how great your plans look, poor presentation will adversely affect their acceptance among your followers.

Many leaders do not see the need to work on their mode of communication, therefore, some of the best and brilliant ideas have been discarded by the followership because they were not properly communicated, and so, were not understood. Alternatively, and sad to say, many worthless ones have been embraced wholeheartedly by the followers just because of the mode of expression from the leaders.

Discussed below are some of the things a leader will need to give attention to, if he wants to learn how to communicate persuasively.

EXPLAIN THE REASONS

If you really need to pass a message across to your followers, and you desire that they carry the instruction out, do not just stop at telling them what you want to do, or what you want them to do in order for you to achieve what you want. You must be ready to emphasize why you want to do what you want to do, and why you also want them to do what you want them to do.

It is not just about telling them what direction to follow, but to convince them that the direction you are leading them through is the best for the moment. Youths naturally will not just do what they are told to do unless they see a reason to. Their inquisitive nature needs to be satisfied. So, give them a reason, let them know why it is important to them. Let everyone see the reason why the decision you are making is of utmost significance to him personally.

“Why” is a great word! The answer to the “why” question can solve a lot of riddles and save a lot of heartaches. It can make some of the most rebellious followers become the most obedient ones. Most youths will do anything if they are convinced that they should do it. They have sharp and organized minds, and are unlikely to revolt against a very reasonable idea, except there is a negative influence over them which is contending for your position. Once you can appeal to their reasoning, you can gain their support.

Be ready to explain things to them in a general assembly. If need be, you may have to explain again to individuals who may feel that they still need more clarifications on it, and who summon the courage to approach you one-on-one about it. Do not take any offence in their boldness. Some others will say worse things behind you that you will never hear off.

It will be a very costly assumption to think that everyone knows why you are embarking on the projects. Do not fall into such an error. Take your time to explain things to them. When youths are not well informed, they become bad followers but when they have accurate information, they become the best supporters which a leader can have.

This is one great undoing of most leaders. They do not take their time to organize their thoughts around the “why”, not to mention communicating these “why”. On the few occasions they do, it is centered on their own short sighted perspective of the issue at hand, and they still expect every youth to reason with them. The followers are not allowed to say anything that will open these thought lines up for scrutiny and proper evaluation.

This is why youths are labeled rebels. They are likely to challenge such leadership. If the leadership, by any means, is able to keep them from challenging his authority, then you can be sure that he has succeeded in creating a gap between his leadership and the followers. If this style continues, the gap will widen by the day until it is obvious to all that the leadership has no strong hold on the followership.

COMMUNICATE PERSUASIVELY

The second thing to do to create instant followership for your visions and dream is to be enthusiastic about your goals. Each time you need to communicate your ideas to them. Do so with all enthusiasm.

Being a leader, you must allow your conviction and your enthusiasm rub off on everyone else. Your passion can become contagious if you really know how to handle it. If you make the best of it, it can give birth to genuine interest and concern in almost all of your followers.

Since the success and the deficiencies of your team will be due to how best you can carry them along at the stage of communicating your ideas to them. Each time you need to communicate with your followers, let your gestures and your speech pattern emphasize your thought line. Communicate in such a way that it will infuse your team with enthusiasm and excitement. In your expressions, let them see much more than you say.

In whatever form of language each one can understand, encourage them to do what they have to do. Do all you can to raise their level of commitment and inspire them to peak performance.

The enthusiasm and vigor with which people seek to accomplish their own dreams cannot be compared with the little they invest in another man’s dream. If you can sell the idea to them and make them believe in it, you will achieve greater success with it in no time.

Do not just make it your idea, make it their idea. After all, it really does not matter whose idea it is. What should matter to you and the organization is the result. With the result, the credit comes to you.

EMPLOY DIPLOMACY

Beyond what you say, you will need to be careful about the way you say it, when you say it, and to whom you say it. Do not just tell it as it is. Be tactful. Tell no lie; but pass the truth across with wisdom. Watch the season so as to know when to say what has to be said. It tells how much acceptance you get.

A single statement can either get you into trouble or get you out of trouble. Whenever you want to speak, make sure what you want to say is worth saying. If not, wait a little longer. Wise men know when to speak, and when not to; how to speak when they have to. Do not be careless: Everything cannot be said at any time and anyhow.

Before embarking on the project of communicating your ideas to your followers (because it is indeed a project on its own), do your homework. Figure out what you have to say before you start to say it. Think through what you want to say before facing the crowd. Think along other people’s thought line; think about their fears, think about their needs, think about the objections they are likely to raise, think about their criticisms and provide ready answers to each of them even before you face them.

This, definitely, will save you from embarrassment when communicating the idea to them. If any of them decides to ask a pre-thought question, you will have a direct, well thought out, less volatile, and honest ready answer to allay their fears, and

consequently portray yourself as a leader who is worth his office.

Remember, you have become a voice for the people who you are leading. Most times, you will have to stand before higher authorities to represent your people and at the same time stand before your people to represent the higher authorities. It is possible for one party to think you are being partisan.

It is even possible, often, that the youths will want to take out their anger for the higher authorities on you, or that the higher authority will want to blame and punish you for the laxities in the actions of the generality of the youths. This is always the case. Therefore, be quick to listen, but be slow to speak.

Even when you are called upon to deliver impromptu speeches, communicate thoughtfully and clearly. Intermittently pause to reflect on the next statement you are about to make. Take your time to calculate the consequence of your words before voicing them out.

BE IN CHARGE OF YOUR TEMPER

Whether in anger or in response to someone in anger, use gentle words! You cannot control the action of others’ but you can control your reaction to their actions. Do not let your temper get the better of you; control yourself.

Raising your voice will not make the youths obey you more than they are willing to. Screaming at them will change nothing. They do not have hearing problems; they only need to be approached the proper way.

For your communication to be effective, it has to get their attention, and for it to get their attention, it has to make sense to them. For it to make sense to them, it has to be said in a way that they will understand. For them to understand well, you need to control your temper.

Stay cool; blowing hot will not make matters easy for you. Either learn to control the manner in which you speak when you are annoyed, or reserve whatever you want to say for a later time.

BE CONFIDENTIAL

As a leader, what you communicate and who you communicate it to will make a lot of difference. There are issues which can only be discussed at meetings with only the relevant team members and stakeholders. Such discussions and decisions made at such meetings may not be meant for public hearing until the time is right.

Your degree of success is determined by how much of such discussions you can keep away from those who it does not concern. Know what to communicate and what not to communicate in the presence of some people.

God gave us two ears but one mouth. This means you are to speak only half as much as you hear. If you do, you will get yourself out of unnecessary troubles. Running a loose mouth is not a quality of a good leader.

Once your followers discover that you do not know how to shut your mouth when you need to, rather than see you as a leader who is interested in their progress, they will conclude that you

have become an enemy of progress to them; and they will treat you accordingly.

Be conscious of your environment at every given time. Always know what to say whenever you are with people.

CLARITY

Unless your communication is clear enough, you risk being misconstrued, misunderstood and misrepresented. This does happen to youth leaders from time to time.

Most times, when we think we are communicating our plans, we do so with all authority, presenting our ideas the best way we can. We seem to be sure of what we are doing, where we are coming from, where we are, and where we are going to. At times, we only know this. Our followers are wondering what we are saying because they do not have a clear grasp of what we mean. The message we send is not the message they receive.

You assume you have passed your intentions across and expect that by now everybody should buy into the vision, but what you have actually done is, if anything, gotten them more confused than they were at the beginning and so, no one seems to want to follow your idea. Then you conclude they are not cooperative and supportive. The problem is not with them, it lies with you. You have not connected with them in the simplest and best way they will understand.

When addressing your followers, be as explicit as possible. Be down to earth. Get everyone to see the larger picture. Communicate your vision to them in such a way that they will adopt it as their own. This will not come easily. You should not

expect that they will naturally begin to care as deeply as you do about your own dream. It will take more than just talking to them about it. It will take real personal convictions. They will have to accept the vision as theirs, embrace it, and run with it; and this only comes when it is well passed across.

In communicating ideas to your followers, simplicity is important. Be specific. Be exact. Be precise. Streamline your thoughts. The more precise you are about your goals, the clearer it will be to them, and the more they will embrace it.

Keeping your choice of words simple will solve a lot of problems during communication. Using big, ambiguous and complex composition of words may sometimes get the audience excited, but they are likely to miscomprehend you once in a while. Learn to speak the right words.

When it comes to youths, just a word can make all the needed difference. A single statement can hold as much wisdom as a long passage, if well-constructed. Whatever information you want to pass across, take your time to choose your words. You may avoid a lot of chaos by doing this.

ADD SOME COLOUR

Your followers do not like to be bored whenever they are in a meeting. This is why each time they have programs of their own, a major part of it will be the side attractions. One of the things they call side attraction is to intentionally look for people to make them laugh; and they pay for it.

This is because they know that most times, their leaders do not share their idea of what catching fun is about, and so will not

make provision for it. So, they invite someone whom their generation calls a stand-up comedian, and pay him heavily just to make them laugh. This shows what importance your followers attach to the issue of catching fun. And if you understand how they feel, you will present your most distasteful dish to them in a capsule they will be willing to swallow.

No one really loves to be made sad; not even the youths. They like fun; add some for them. This does not mean you have to water down whatever you want to say.

Once in a while, when occasions allow for it, do something different, and do it in a creative way. Use an audio-visual aid or a projection slide to enhance your communication. Throw a nice complement. Share a joke. Tell a short story. Make an illustration to drive home your point. Say something funny. Whatever you are going to do, make it relevant to your general presentation for that day. It keeps them glued to you whenever you are talking, and they keep looking forward to the next meeting when they will have the opportunity to listen to you again.

Once you get to this level with your followers, there is little or nothing you cannot achieve with them.

LACE IT WITH WISDOM

Whatever color you add to your words, your words will not carry weight unless they are words of wisdom. Each time you want to say a thing, make sure your words are words of knowledge.

The Bible says wisdom is the principal thing, and also advises that in all thy getting, get understanding. This sums it all.

Always communicate in wisdom. Once they see your level of understanding and maturity in the display of wisdom each time you speak on general issues, they will begin to bring very personal issues to you for counsel and clarification. This, is a sign of great respect for the grace of great wisdom in display each time you speak.

YOUTHS GENERAL REACTION TO LEADERSHIP

Youth leadership is about one of the most complex organizations in the society. It is harder to lead than most others. Some people may feel you are too hard at a time when others complain you are too weak. This can be confusing.

You may feel that people should generally feel enthusiastic about the ideas you have which you think will move your organization forward, but you will be surprised that some will still resent your leadership.

There five types of followers every leader will have to contend with. There are those who:

1. RESPECT LEADERSHIP

The first group consists of those who respect leadership. They do their best to support the leadership. They never cause chaos or initiate problems. They are the easy going followers. Most times, you may even take them for granted because they never complain. They may be called zombies, not because they are

daft, but because they are always comfortable with whatever decision the leadership takes, good or bad.

2. ENDURE THE LEADERSHIP

The second group consists of those who endure the leadership. These ones too, although as easy going as the first group, are not as quiet as they are. These ones complain when they find things they are not comfortable with. They do not make trouble, but they will let the leadership know that they are not in support of their actions.

Once the leadership is insistent in doing what these ones do not like, the only thing they do is keep quiet, keep low, and keep praying that its time will soon be up. They endure the leadership until that prayer is answered. Most times the answer comes faster than they can imagine.

All leaders must be careful with this group of people. They may not be troublemakers, but their advice is worth much more than you can imagine.

3. INFLUENCE LEADERSHIP

This third group consists of people who want to become an influence over the leadership. They seem to, indirectly, control the affairs of the group by dictating to the leadership what they feel needs to be done per time.

This may be because of their influence on the group, or on the leader, or their influence on the committee or group that selected or elected the leader into position. They feel they are still in charge and should be heard. Some of them even feel that the

leader should seek permission from them before doing anything in office.

The earlier a leader learns to dissociate from such people, the better for him. Most times, they become a clog in the wheel of his progress.

4. FIGHT LEADERSHIP

This is one of the most problematic groups. They are the trouble makers. All they do is look for reasons to foment trouble. If there is none, they create one. They intentionally set traps for the leader so they will have opportunity to pick quarrels with him.

Once you identify these ones, keep as far away as possible from them. Nothing good will come from your relationship with them.

5. CHANGE THE LEADERSHIP

The last group is those who are not interested in the leadership because he is not one of their own people. All they do is with the single intention to remove him from leadership, so that they can put one of their own.

These five groups of people every leader will find among his followers. So be prepared to deal with each accordingly. Once you can get maximum co-operation from a larger percentage of each group, you are bound to succeed. The first thing you will need to do is to identify them.

In addition to these five, there are two other sets of people whom your success is hinged on.

1. THOSE YOU DEPEND ON

These are the people whom your success as a leader is dependent on something which they have to do during your tenure. Doing what they have to do may not affect you directly, but will still contribute to the final success which you envisage. If these ones fail to do these, you are likely to be affected adversely.

Once you identify them, know how to deal with them. Monitor them from time to time to ensure that they do what they should do when they have to do it. Make their problem your problem, because it is indeed your problem.

These can be the people on your executive board, your departmental leaders or your workforce.

2. THOSE THAT DEPEND ON YOU

These ones are the exact opposite of the other one. What you are to the group above, these ones are to you. In the other group, you were the dependant, but in this group they are the dependants. Their success lies solely on something which you have to do. If you fail to do it, you may jeopardize their success.

You also need to learn how to deal with them. To a large extent, they will be at your mercy, and you can decide to do whatever you will with them. You call the shots here. However, you must remember that whatever goes round comes round. However well or badly you treat the people who depend on you, God will make sure that those you depend on also treat you in like manner.

Make it very easy for the people who need your favor to get it, and those you need their favor will not deny you.
