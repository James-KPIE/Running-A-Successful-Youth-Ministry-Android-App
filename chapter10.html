Chapter Ten

CONFLICT MANAGEMENT SKILLS

To be successful as a leader, you need to learn conflict management skills. Great skills will be required to handle your followers, especially the troublesome ones, particularly during the time of crisis.

Settling quarrels and disputes is about reconciliations with friends and re-establishing relationships. It is about resolving issues and not dissolving relationship. Sharing blames most times will dissolve relationship.

Each misunderstanding and disagreement must be addressed at an accurate time. Each crisis should be attended to in a timely manner. Timely in this sense does not only mean urgency, although at times, this may be the case, but some cases may require time; give such time.

At times, chastisement may be necessary, and where this is the case, wisdom must be applied. Manage every conflict confidently and constructively; do not be defensive.

SETTLING DISPUTES BETWEEN PARTIES

From time to times, you may find yourself in a position of settling disputes or managing crisis between two followers. There are five major steps to take before taking any action in this regard:-

1. HEAR BOTH PARTIES OUT

Never base your judgment on the narration from one of the two parties involved; this will make you a partisan leader and thus

can devalue the respect you enjoy with the youths. Even if you have an interest to protect, either in the case under investigation or in one of the parties involved, you do not have to make it obvious. Get detailed information of events from all of the parties involved without being unnecessarily or hastily judgmental.

2. HEAR THEIR WITNESSES

If need be, hear their witnesses too. No one in his right senses will narrate a story that will implicate him. It is natural that the stories of all of the parties involved will be so polished that it may be difficult to pin any offence on any of them by simply listening to their narrations alone. They would have buried the unsatisfactory part and would only emphatically pronounce the side of the story that portrays them as saints. The essence of this, either intentionally or not, is to derail you. It is natural; nobody likes being condemned. This is why you need more information - something more than what the hurting parties are willing to divulge; and the witnesses are your best bet.

3. ASK QUESTIONS

Thirdly, ask questions. After listening to all the stories, you surely will have some contrasting facts for which clarifications will be needed. For such, ask relevant questions that will expose the true situation.

4. PONDER THOUGHTFULLY

Ponder thoughtfully on the information you have gathered. Re-examine the stories and try to understand the issues at hand. Do not be in a hurry to resolve the issue or to condemn anyone. Examine the responses to your questions until you are sure you

have an understanding of the complexity of the situation at hand.

5. SETTLE THE DISPUTE AMICABLY

Lastly settle the dispute amicably. Except there is a guiding law in the organization that says such offences must be punished, do not give any punishment. Remember, it is just a misunderstanding between two followers, and not a serious crime. Take no serious disciplinary action. Treat everyone fairly. Learn to identify the problems without accusing anyone. Always seek to be a reconciler in such situations. Let those who are right know they are, and those who are wrong know as well without condemning any of the parties involved. Your intention should be to settle the dispute and not to rekindle it. So, handle the situation with care. If you need to condemn the erring party, you can do that at a later time, and in the absence of others, when the issue has subsided. If you must discipline or chastise, do it compassionately, with all honesty and with no hidden motives.

SETTLING PERSONAL DISPUTES

Described above is the manner in which you can settle disputes between two followers. It is also possible that the crisis maybe between you and one of your followers, or may even be between you and the totality of the youth body. Such a crisis must be carefully handled so that it does not degenerate further as this can lead into a breakdown of law and order.

If you ever find yourself in a situation such as that, do not let others discover and reveal your faults before you admit them. You are not a god. If you cover up your mistakes and they get to discover later from other sources, they may become disappointed in you. Detecting your own faults and admitting

them openly before those who need to know (and not just every Jack and Harry) will save you open disgrace and the public embarrassment that will follow if your followers discover, on their own, what you are trying to cover up. Be very open.

Do not fight to, unnecessarily, cover up your mistakes. If you cover your own mistakes, how do you intend to talk about those of others? Be honest. Let them know you are human and, therefore, prone to error. If, in all sincerity, you are at fault, admit it. Accept your mistakes. You will also gain more respect from the people you are leading.

Rather than acknowledge their faults, some leaders will try to cover up their inefficiency by blaming others for their actions. Never blame another for the problem you caused. If you keep blaming others for your failures, you are likely to keep insisting that you are right. This way, you will not see the need for personal development.

Take responsibility for your actions and your decisions. Do not try to shift the blame onto others and do not try to hide it from them. Admitting your errors shows you are ready to learn. And that is the hallmark of a good leader.

Demonstrate courage during such time of adversity. There will always be a time of crisis. If you breakdown at such times, it shows you are not man enough.

DEALING WITH INSULTS

Some youths (your followers), because of the age and time that we are in, lack good home upbringing and so, do not have good morals. Some of them can speak rebelliously to their parents.

Some can even shout their parents down and hurl insults at them.

If you are lucky enough (or rather, unlucky) to have some of them among your followers, you will need to handle them with care. Someone who does not see anything wrong with screaming at his parents will not see anything wrong with insulting a mere youth leader who he feels needs to be corrected.

There are so many issues which we have no control over. These include the tone of the voice of our followers when they are annoyed, the style of presentation of their arguments, their gesticulations and the likes. Although we may not be able to control their actions, we should be able to control our own reactions. How we respond to their accusations, their presentation notwithstanding, will go a long way to determining whether we are mature leaders or not.

Accept insults in good faith. This is not to encourage the youths to abuse you at will or to take you for granted. It is because you have to keep the unity of the body in mind. This is one of the sacrifices you will need to make. The unity of the group you are called to lead is more important than your personal ego.

This is why you are advised to be careful not to intentionally do anything that may give any of the youths the freedom to insult you. Youths too are human, and are bound to be very volatile when angered. So, tread gently.

Conversely, no matter how gently you tread, some bad eggs among them will still step on your toes. If you are a youth leader, considering the fact that the age difference between you

and your followers in most cases will not be that much to warrant a high degree of respect, such occurrences cannot be found wanting, and some devil-inspired youths are waiting for such moments to take the advantage, so as to spite you. Your ability to handle such situations maturely will be proof of the type of leader that you are.

If any of the youths you are leading annoyingly insults you publicly in a moment of rage, whether you are the cause of his infuriation or not, be willing to control yourself, overcome your pride and swallow the reproach. It is not a time to begin to flex muscles or exchange insults openly. You do not have to prove that you are the leader by serving him back in his own plate. You prove you are the leader by being cool, calm and collected. Believe me; it takes a whole lot of maturity to do this.

Avoid being abusive. Never respond emotionally or prematurely to situations. Learn not to get upset easily. Do not let the situation dictate your emotion; you control the situation. Every baseless critical comment aimed at you should be waved aside. If you concentrate much on your criticsâ€™ complaints, you will become destabilized and will lose concentration on the major task at hand. Irrespective of what happens, never lose control of your emotions. Be calm always. Never raise a hand at anyone: to slap, beat, or exchange blows. Control yourself.

Some of these youths, unknown to you, might have tried other means to get at you without much success, and this opportunity is what they have been waiting for, to abuse your office and drag your name in the mud. Most of the unkind words they will use are not spontaneous! They have rehearsed them over and

over again in the privacy of their rooms, and they know what next to say to each reaction to their accusations.

If you react to their emotional display the way you naturally should, your reaction will be unplanned and impromptu. Since they already have a game plan, you will fall into their traps. So, keep your reactions under check.

All they want to do is disrespect you and cause you to misbehave in the presence of other youths so they may have something tangible against you. Disappoint them. Be calm. Accept the insults in good faith. Never be instigated to raise your voice, exchange words, or lift your hands against them. If you do, you will spoil more things before you realize it.

The initial reaction will be to feel disappointed, depressed and discouraged. You may even be tempted to feel annoyed and embittered. It may even be a mixture of these two reactions - sadness and madness. No matter how you feel, do not allow your emotions to get the better of you.

Do not foment trouble. Do not intentionally provoke crisis. Whatever excuse you have for causing trouble will not be in your best interest at the end of the day. You will regret ever doing it. Without activating any more problems to yourself, you certainly will have enough of them on your hands. Each leader has his fill of crisis without looking for it. Why ask for more? Avoid all avoidable troubles. Only confront the few that cannot be avoided.

You may want to address the issue of respect and manners, but this will achieve better results if it is done at a later time, and not

in this meeting. If you feel a particular youth is doing this, as you leave the place of the meeting, you may consider reporting such to the appropriate quarters for adequate disciplinary measures to be taken so that it will serve as a lesson for others who may want to tow his line.
