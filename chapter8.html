Chapter Eight

DELEGATE RESPONSIBILITY

One way in which you can explore and maximize the potentials in your followers is to delegate responsibilities to them.

Successful leadership among youth requires the combined efforts of almost all the youth and their abilities. In youth leadership, there is no one-man-army or show. It is more of a general contribution of each followers little effort. Everyone needs to be involved. It is not all about you and your vision; your people must be included.

In leading the youth, leadership is not something that is done by the leader for the followers. It is done, although by the leader, but with the co-operation and help of the led. There is at least something which everyone can contribute.

Cross pollinate ideas with other people. No leader succeeds alone. None of us is as good as all of us put together. If successful leadership is about intelligence, the most intelligent guys around the world would be the best leaders. This is rarely the case. The best leaders are not the most intellectual ones around, they are simply men who could work well with people and get the best out of them, with ease.

Leadership is not all about intelligence or brilliance. It is more about understanding the feelings of others and knowing how to influence them. Your ability to get other youth to work with you, using their unique abilities, will make you a successful leader more than anything else.

You are only as good as the people you surround yourself with. The greatest leaders in history are not those who force, demand or insist, but those who suggest, motivate, and encourage others to act. They surround themselves with, get advices and suggestions from the best of the best, and so produce the best leadership. If you want to be recognized as one of the greatest leaders around, you will also need to learn how to delegate responsibilities to the best brains around you.

Like attracts like! The people whom your leadership will attract will most likely be men who have some character traits and talents similar to yours; maximize their abilities by involving them. Make use of the people who are ready to contribute their quota in fulfilling the joint dream of the organization.

Some youth are reserved. They may not freely express their abilities or demonstrate their talents until they are laden with responsibilities. Involve them by making them responsible for something; give them a responsibility.

It is tough to be a youth leader today, probably tougher than it has ever been. Leadership responsibilities are overwhelmingly exhaustive. True leaders face heavier demands every day because their schedules are so tight with the number of things they have to personally attend to. It will require serious discipline for anyone to take his eyes off some of those duties

that stare him in the face. Most of these are really not as important as they seem. They just distract the leader from the more important things by wasting his time and energy.

If you really want to leave the office in one piece, you will need to discipline yourself enough to take your eyes off responsibilities that others can take care of for you. One of the major causes of burnout in leadership is the desire to want to do it all alone. Most times, leaders concern themselves with what others should do and they neglect what they should do. Do not put too many irons in the fire at the same time, it is distracting.

In order to avoid these distractions, any activity that is not moving you closer to your goals must be eliminated. If it cannot be eliminated, then it should be suspended. If it, necessarily, has to be done immediately, delegate it to someone else. Rather than be a hard working leader, be a smart working leader. Identify what is important for you to do and delegate the rest. Look for your own area of giftedness and major on it, encouraging others to give helping hands in other areas.

If it is something you and you alone must do, postpone it. This done, you can do it in your least productive time and have your most productive time for your number one priority. This way, you can be a specialist in your own field and also oversee others in their fields.

The multitudinous dimension of the task ahead will be too difficult for you alone to bear. You need helpmates. This is the reality of life. In life, the higher you go, the more dependent you are on others. A seventy-year old man will depend on the people around him if he will enjoy the remaining years of his life. The

things which he had always done with ease by himself and for himself before now will be difficult to achieve. It may become a suicidal mission if he attempts to do them all by himself.

The same is true of the business world. A CEO depends on his cook, cobbler, gateman, laundry man and the like to live a good life. A multibillionaire will depend on the trickles of money being made by some unknown and insignificant workers in his firm to maintain his billions. The moment they all go on strike, his business is affected. He cannot, single handedly, undertake everyone’s task; it will be a suicide mission.

The same is true of a leader. If you want to be in charge of everything, every time, you will kill yourself before long. Science has revealed to us that even the sun increases its brightness and intensity by shedding a part of its mass every day. You also will need to shed some weight of responsibilities. The task before you is greater than what you alone can accomplish.

It really does not matter what organization you are leading. If you are a youth minister in a church, a singles’ coordinator, a student union leader, a youth executive or a campus leader, and everyone in your team contributes maximally to the fulfillment of your dream, the progress you will experience will be immeasurable. This is what successful leadership is all about: getting others interested in the joint goal of the house for the progress of the organization and keeping them that way till the goal is achieved.

Every leader needs help. There is simply no way one man can do it all alone. You cannot make any meaningful changes all by

yourself. There are numerous others who can do what you cannot. Allow them. That is the way God created the world to be. Everyone needs to do, at least, something for someone in order for everyone to be happy. That is the way to get jobs done.

You were not ordained a leader to do the impossible, but to make possible the possible. If all leaders can get this into their heads, there will be a large reduction in the unnecessary pressure which they cause themselves. Know what you can handle yourself, and what you cannot handle. If you know that you cannot handle a thing, seek the expertise of others in the group who you can delegate it to. This will not diminish you in any way. You are just a leader, not the omnipotent God.

Being the leader, your most valuable assets are the people around you. The best solutions to the greatest problems you will have, either in your personal life or in carrying out your official duties are in the people around you; make use of them. We all need one another. You need them as much as they need you. So, involve them. Involving them will give them the experience of personal joy of service. With the genuine involvement of everyone, there is going to be corporate accomplishment.

It is not always so easy to leave the most important thing as the most important and delegate others. So, you will have to learn to. The secret has always been to do the important things first, leaving the less important ones for others to do. People want to belong; other youths want to participate actively; so, give them room. You major on the majors and minor on minors. This way, they can major on the minors, and the results will be all majors.

Delegate responsibilities. Put others to work. Be informed about all their activities, but save yourself the full and direct involvement in all their operations. You should have more important things to do. If you, really, do not have to be there, you do not need to be there. If you, really, do not have to do it personally, save yourself the stress.

Designate responsibility and watch the members of your team work, encouraging them and monitoring them when you need to. This way, they can learn on the job and become better at it with time. That is the best way for a leader to lead.

Most of the things which you think you only can achieve is because you have not given other people the opportunity to try their hands on them. If you do, you may discover that some of them even have the tendency to do those things better than you, if allowed to perfect the skills. Things may not be done the way you desired at the initial stage, but you must accept the best which people have to offer as their best, encouraging them to improve where possible without too many complaints, and give them the opportunity to improve without nursing unrealistic expectations of them. No one is perfect. Accept them the way they are now. With time, you will be proud of them.

Giving yourself to constantly condemning the best effort of other people may be very discouraging. Learn to appreciate a near-perfect job. This is not being hypocritical. It is maturity. You are not deceiving them, you are appreciating what they have done, and at the same time telling them they can still do better.

To start with, get the best of the people in your group to work with you. Give room to other youths who have strength in the areas where you are weak. It will not remove anything from you, it can only add to you.

If they handle designated functions better than you would have done and it becomes obvious to the populace, it is natural to begin to nurse the thoughts that they are trying to compete with you. This early sign of jealousy is not unusual, but should not be allowed to go beyond this stage. If you are indeed a good leader you should appreciate them and give them the healthy respect and the credit which is duly theirs without being envious of them. If you do this, they will be more submissive than you expect and the result will be to the glory of your administration and the credit to your leadership. If you start competing with them, you may cause more problems than you can handle. Such problems can discredit in few weeks, your many years of meritorious service and commitment to the organization.

Encourage co-operation and team work among your immediate co-workers. It makes much sense to assign particular duties to each of your members, although some should get more, some others less. Youths are very active and impatient. If you leave them with nothing to do, they naturally become restless, and soon get out of hand, needing to be controlled. Look for something each one can do, no matter how little. Commend the willing, encourage the unwilling, ensure everybody participates. Delegating responsibilities is a demonstration of your trust in them and their abilities. It also will build a great working team.

However, in the name of encouraging participation, do not overburden your followers. Make as few demands on them as

possible. Do not overstretch them Allow them stretch themselves to a reasonable extent if they so desire, but do not force them to.

DELEGATE AUTHORITY

Do not only delegate responsibilities, also delegate authority. In fact, never delegate responsibility without delegating proportionate authority. Responsibility that is not backed up with reasonable and adequate authority will end each attempt at excellence in futility. Each time you delegate a responsibility, to make it effective, delegate appreciable authority alongside.

Do not hoard power. Share authority with your followers. Once you delegate responsibility, equip them with the needed authority to carry out the responsibility. When you delegate authority, you empower your delegates to perform. The delegated authority serves as an enablement to manifest.

This practice of releasing some level of authority to your followers will not make you less authoritative. Every time you hold tenaciously to power, rather than portray you as some one who really cares for the progress of all and who has the best interest of the group at heart, you are seen as a greedy and selfish leader who wants to only boss it over all. If you feel comfortable when you are the only one in charge, you are not matured enough to be the great leader whom the youths desire. You are a boss, and a boss is more of a dictator than a leader.

This generation of youths will likely not respond to a dictatorial style of leadership. In the past, the majority of the youths were known (or rather forced) to be silent, leaving the decision making to the few in leadership and the executives. Now, almost

everyone wants to have a say. The earlier you understand this, and better still, embrace it, the more successful you are likely to become as a leader.

If you are truly a leader, you do not have to be in control all the time. Once in a while, you surrender control. You give your trusted hands some level of freedom to take some minor decisions in your absence and to take charge of their positions of responsibility authoritatively. If you delegate authority this way; it will come back to you in multiple folds.

Trust the people who are handling your projects. Mistrust destroys unity and impedes progress. Keep your eyes on, but your hands off the responsibilities of the leaders under you, as long as they are not failing in their duties. Monitor them closely, but give them enough freedom to do it the best way they can.

The more the authority you delegate to others, the more respected you become and the more successful your regime becomes. Power brings much more profit and lasts longer when it is used to empower others.
