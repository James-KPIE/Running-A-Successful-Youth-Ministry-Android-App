Chapter Four

RESPONSIBILITIES OF A YOUTH LEADER (3)

Beside your responsibilities to the people who you are leading, your primary responsibility as a leader is first, to yourself. You cannot fulfill your responsibilities to others unless you have first satisfied your responsibility to yourself. So, it makes sense to know what your responsibilities to yourself are, and to attend to them before anything else.

BUILD UP THEIR TRUST IN YOU
One thing you cannot do without is the ability to make people like you. This is crucial! Whatever the level of your managerial ability and management techniques, if your followers do not like you, you may not succeed.

Today’s youths may not totally trust you or your sense of reasoning and judgment as readily and as much as you will desire. You will need to do all possible to build up the confidence you expect them to have in you. Do this one step at a time. This is necessary because if they do not love you, they cannot love your vision. They will have to accept you before they accept your plans, no matter how mouth-watering it may look.

So, your first responsibility to yourself as a leader is to find a way to build up trust in your followers. Start up on this good note: win their hearts over. This will naturally increase the confidence your followers have in you. If you can win their hearts at the initial stage, you are likely to win their allegiance and support all through your tenure as leader. Youths are so easy to predict. If they love you; they love you. Even if you mistakenly misbehave, they are willing to overlook it, forgive you and embrace you back. If they hate you from the start, it will take some time for them to change their minds about you, even if you do the right things!

Your main point of focus here should be how to establish credibility and earn their trust. These may take a lot of outward expression of care, concern, patience, love, wisdom and the like. The extent to which they can trust you is the extent to which they will follow you. So, develop friendship skills with them now before it is too late, else, you will be deficient in your ability to relate with them and carry them along in your bid to lead aright, and this will greatly hamper your efficiency as a leader.

UPGRADE YOURSELF
How much of what you are doing today do you really know? Can what you know about what you are doing move the group higher? If the answer is no, then upgrade yourself!

Those you lead will not respect your leadership if they think they know much more than you do! Who wants to be led by a

fool? If your followers know far more than you, it is not likely they will be willing to follow you. You really may not be the most intelligent in the group you lead, but you really do not have to be the least. You must know, at least, more than an average member of the group, else you are not worthy to be called their leader.
The most interesting group of people to lead today is youths. The advancement in information technology has opened them up to a wealth of information. This information is readily available at their fingertips. They already have an idea about how well to do what you intend to do. If your approach to it is still crude and uncivilized, and you are not willing to learn, you will soon lose their allegiance to you. Two things which will readily help you in dealing with them are the information you have access to and the grace of God upon your life. These two will work hand in hand; the presence of one will not make up for the absence of the other.

You will be tempted beyond measure to focus more on what you do, to the neglect of the new information and knowledge you should acquire. This, if allowed, will transmit into grievous consequences in the nearest future. Go for relevant up-to-date information so that you will not remain out-of-date. If you are not updated, you will be outdated in no time.

Strive to know more than the people you claim to lead. Keep awake in the night to upgrade yourself. A wise man said: “If you sleep when your followers are sleeping, you soon will become one of the followers, and one of the followers will take your place”. Do not be a Martha who is always running around to see
that things fall in place. Be a Mary who will take out time to learn so as to improve herself.

All over the world, big companies set time and allocate resources for the development of their managerial staff. They take them through trainings and seminars to get the best out of them. You will also need to develop yourself to remain the leader of the group you are leading. If you give continually without replenishing, you will soon exhaust yourself and whittle out.

Have a teachable spirit. You will not excel simply by desiring to. Learn! Sharpen your skills. Improve yourself. Learn more. Learn a little more. Learn some more. And then learn much more. Training gives rise to confidence. The more training you get the more confident you are to carry out your leadership responsibilities and the better you will be at it.

Give yourself enough trainings as much as time will allow you: formal trainings, informal trainings, workshops, seminars, conferences, and other such personal development projects. Learn from books. Learn from other youths. Learn from good political leaders. Learn from religions leaders. Learn from your role models and learn from life itself.

These trainings should not just be in the area of your strength alone, but also in the area of your weaknesses. It is natural to want to upgrade yourself in the area of your strength alone. Do not do this to the neglect of your weaknesses.

Every leader gravitates in the direction of their strengths, for that is where they get the best result and the loudest applause.

However, upgrading yourself in the area of your weaknesses too will make you a balanced leader and your flaws and weaknesses will not be too pronounced to your followers.

Always do this from time to time. There is no such thing as one-time-learning. The world is improving by the day, technology is advancing by the minute, and ideas are progressing rapidly. Do not be left behind. The longer you stay in the position of leadership, the busier you get, and the more exhausted you become. So, do not forget to build your self up from time to time so as to sharpen your leadership skill.

BE COMMITTMENT TO YOUR OWN GOALS
A leader’s seriousness is determined by his degree of performance at the task assigned, irrespective of the challenges he has. Therefore, your commitment to your task must not be conditional. It must be without any limit.

Regardless of the number of the people you are leading, your followers deserve your best. Put in your best by being totally committed to the task before you. Do not expect any of your followers to be committed to your vision if you are not wholly committed to it yourself. Great leaders encourage other people’s commitment by their own commitment. Jim Rohn once quoted his mentor: “My mentor said “let’s go do it’, not “you go do it”. How powerful when someone says, “let’s”!

Be a role model for your followers by being the first to take the lead. What you are going to demand from them you must be ready to give yourself. If you demand honesty, be honest. If you demand fairness, be fair. If you demand openness, be open. If you demand commitment, be committed. If you demand
dedication, be dedicated. If you demand financial sacrifice, as much as it lies within your capacity, take the lead. It is tit for tat. Do not demand much from your followers if you are not ready to sacrifice much yourself.

Do not just give orders, participate. “Talk” is cheap. Anyone can talk, but not everyone will be willing to act. Do not just fold your hands, sit down and expect to be worshipped. Always take the lead in pursuing the primary goal at hand. Absolute commitment to the primary vision is the hallmark of any great leader. Throw all of your energy in the direction of your goal, and you will discover that others will follow, doggedly. Staying focused on your goal will make you more productive and keep your people tightly glued to you.

President Dwight Eisenhower once demonstrated this using a simple piece of thread thrown on a table. When he pulled it, it followed him everywhere he wanted, but when he pushed it, it went nowhere. In leadership, men are like strings. If you lead the way and pull them along, all or at least most follow suit, but if you push, you go alone.

There will be enough distractions, but be faithful to your responsibilities. Being the kind of people they are, some youths may accuse you of selfish interests. Some higher authorities who do not understand why you do what you do may label you as an unserious leader, but be determined. Do not allow their baseless words distract you from achieving carefully laid down goals. If, before embarking on the project, you really take your time to deliberate on the actions and the challenges to expect, then see to its fulfillment. The end will justify the means.

To keep you away from some of these distractions, you will need undivided concentration. Some of the excuses of your distractors may be genuine; they all may not stem from a root of jealousy or envy. Some of them may come out of sincere love and concern for you and the projects you are handling. The problem, however, is that they may not be seeing from your own point of view and as such, are bound to disagree with your mode of operation.

You may decide to bring such youths closer to you, dialogue with them and make them see reason with you. Rather than allow them to say all manner of things behind you and corrupt the other youths, you may decide to harmonize some of their thoughts with your common goals, as long as it does not affect your main dream adversely. If you can win them over, they will be a strong instrument to channel your course among the other youths.

This singular action, the process of winning to yourself the major opposition group among your youths, will be interpreted as good leadership that has the ability to handle the youths aright. You will score more points this way.

BE A FOLLOWER
Although you are a leader, and you have a number of other followers who appreciate your leadership, in order to be a good leader, you also need to be a good follower. You need leaders who will lead you.

Life is give and take. You have been giving out instructions by teaching and leading others, you also will need to take in. You need mentors to refill, not just mentors but experienced mentors.

You need someone to constantly encourage and counsel you. You need someone you are answerable to. Without this, you being a leader, will be your own boss, without any checks and balances.

It is imperative that you are accountable to someone. Look for leaders you want to be like and endeavor to develop a mentoring relationship with them. There is no substitute for superior experience. There are so many things you cannot afford to learn through personal experience. Your mentor can teach you those.

Listen to those who have more insight than you do. Keep close to those who are more successful than you are. Every new generation should be an improvement on the former. To achieve this, the new generation needs to move closer to the outgoing generation and observe the best of their leaders closely. Learn from their achievements and their failures. Listen to good advice from them and model the positive principles you see in them. Put serious effort into incorporating their leadership styles, godly values and essential qualities into your life.

During your moment of crisis, seek out help from other experienced and respected leaders around you. There is hardly a problem or challenge you encounter that someone has not already handled. Just look for the person and download the solution from him.

More often than not, the people who you really desire to be your mentors may always be the busiest around, and will have little or no time for you. You can study their lives from afar and pattern your life after theirs. Study their materials. Very soon, you will become like them.

In following other leaders as your mentors, please bear in mind that someone else’ techniques may not always produce exactly the same results it produced somewhere else in another particular setting. People are not the same everywhere. Character, charisma, abilities and leadership qualities differ. If you want to introduce something which you learnt from a mentor to your followers, make sure it will be relevant to your people.

BE YOURSELF
You may not find this last advice very encouraging; but you will need it. Be yourself. Do not try to impress your youths by being another person. If they wanted that person they would go for him. The person they want is you; that is why they have tabernacle with you. Do not deny them the person their hearts desire.

There will always be a better way of doing things so that you can become a better you. Learn that better way without losing your personality. Become a better person but do not lose what makes you unique.

Youths listen to a lot of leaders apart from their own direct leaders. They will make recommendations to you. “Why don’t you dress like Mr. A, or talk like Mrs. B, or counsel like Dr. C, or walk like Prof. D?” There will always be a basis for comparison with another person. Make up your mind to only learn what you do not know and not to lose what you have.
