Chapter Three

RESPONSIBILITIES OF A YOUTH LEADER (2)

If you are a leader, lead and keep on leading. What will show to all that you are really leading is your ability to carry out all the responsibilities effectively. Do not just resume in the office, assume the responsibilities of the office.

Having enumerated the three major needs of a youth in the first chapter, the major responsibility of any youth leader then will be to meet these paramount needs of the youth. Anyone, leader or follower, who meets these needs is automatically seen by the youths as their leader.

One surefire way to meet all of these needs put together is by showing you care for them.

SHOW YOU CARE
I have stumbled on this statement a number of times and I believe what it says. Its truth is relevant to the issue under consideration; people do not care how much you know until they know how much you care.

All youth leaders are encouraged to write this out in black on a plain white sheet and paste it right beside their bedside where it is seen every morning. Leadership is not, most times, about how much you know but how much you care.

The unending quest for knowledge in most youth gatherings may make the leadership mantle always fall on the most knowledgeable one around, without minding how little he cares. However, as soon as this leader who has been chosen based on his knowledge and ability steps into the position of leadership, more will be demanded from him than he can offer. His success is now measured not only by how much he knows, but also by how much he cares; and if he fails in the latter, no matter his degree of success in the former, he will end up having problems with a majority of his followers.

Most leaders sincerely feel that placing such a high demand of care on them, coupled with all the selfless efforts and other contributions they have to make for the advancement of the unit, is like asking for too much. So, if you feel the same way, you are not alone. Others before you have felt the same; and countless number of others coming after you will still feel the same. Their feelings have never changed the people’s opinion; neither will it ever change it. The only solution is to be ready to meet up with the demands by showing to your followers that you indeed care.

Your relationship with every one of your followers is important. Men are naturally drawn to those who show them love. They easily flow with those who care about them and are always deeply committed to those who are concerned about their personal progress.
Having a personal relationship with them enhances their contribution to your endeavors. Your focus should not be mainly on the project but on the students you use to carry out the project. They should be more important to you than the project because without them, hardly can the project move.

If leadership is all about people, then the key to successful leadership will be learning to get along with people, caring for them, and caring about them. If you are only committed to the success of your goals, and are not committed to the people you intend to use to accomplish the goals, you will most likely, meet a brick wall as time goes on. The more progressive your people get under you administration, the more interested they get in your dreams. The more committed and devoted they become in your dream, the more success your regime achieves.

The foundational principle for leadership is love and care for followers. Every other thing depends on it. If you really want to influence men or you desire that they follow as you lead, first make a personal investment in their lives, and you will see them glued to you and your dream. If you can succeed in making them realize how important they are to you first, getting them to realize how important they are to your dream will be easy.

Some of the most gifted and most talented people around have difficulties leading others because they have very poor human relations. They rely on their gifts and talents so much that they neglect the place of human resources. This always tells adversely at a later time in their lives. If you want to get along with the youths of today, you need an amiable and caring personality.

Do not care more about power than people. Care less about how much authority you have, and much more about how many lives you can impact. No one ever regrets showing love in the long run.

One major advantage of showing love to them is that they are able to put up with your faults and shortcomings and tolerate your mistakes and failures or probably overlook them.

Another is that it will also make them more submissive to your authority. It is quite hard to argue with people who really love you. You only force yourself into such arguments when it becomes really necessary. The respect which the person has earned himself by showing love to you makes it almost impossible for you to want to have any serious argument with him. If you show your followers genuine care and love, you will get them more committed to you.

The youth leader’s best assets are people. Nothing can be done without them. Be friendly to them. Be helpful. Be cordial. Have a good inter-personal relationship. Do not only be interested in what they can contribute to your goals. Be genuinely interested in them too, especially your immediate work force. If this is not done, they may begin to see you as a selfish leader who is only interested in his own goals alone, for his own selfish interest and hidden agenda.
Spend more time in developing good rapport with people. Leadership is all about relating with others. When you relate

well, you become a good leader. If you do not, you are termed the opposite.
Build deep relationships with your followers by spending time with them. To be loved, you love first. Your success is deeply rooted in your willingness to invest time in establishing cordial relationships.

HOW TO SHOW YOU CARE
The level of care and concern youths expect today from their leaders today is higher than it was in previous generations. To lead youths in this generation, you need both a hard mind and a soft heart. The hard mind will help you lead them through the turbulent waters and the soft heart will help you to get along with your people successfully.

In this age, you cannot afford to lead from afar, else you will lose the people you are meant to lead. You need to move close to them; get to know them’ know their needs; be genuinely interested in their interests too. You will operate better this way.

These few advices below will help you to show the needed care to the youths you have been called to lead.

BE A GOOD LISTENER
Most of the youths who come to you with their problems really do not need a miracle-working problem solver; all they need is a listening hear. And this is what they lack.

Many of them have issues and great secrets to share, but they cannot confide in their parents at home for fear of being crucified. Their lecturers in school behave as though they are gods and their offices are no go areas for any personal issue. The closest of their friends and classmates will not take them seriously, and may even mock them later. So, they have no confidants. Be their confidant. Whether you can help them or not, do them the honor of listening whenever they need someone to talk to.

Silence is a great part of communication. You really do not have to fill every vacuum in a discussion with words. You do not have to talk unless you need to. So many of them will get a lot of relief simply by talking to you and knowing that you are listening to all they have to say; you will be more loved than ever before.
Being a good listener is the key to a cordial relationship between leaders and followers. On your own part, listening will give you a better understanding of what they are going through. On their own part, it increases their trust in you. If man needs training to be a good speaker, then he definitely needs training to be a good listener. Both do not come naturally.

So, train yourself to be a good speaker if need be. When listening, hear more than the words. Pay attention to people’s tones and other nonverbal behaviors.
One quality of a good listener is that he knows the appropriate responses. The response may not be in words alone. It could be a smile, a grin, a wink, a sigh, a pat on the back, or on the shoulder, a hug and other non-vocal actions too numerous to mention. All these are skills that need to be learnt with time if you must be a successful leader.

If you eventually talk at the end of the day, some of them will have solutions to their problems by listening to what you have to say, and they will attribute these solutions to you.

If you will take the time needed to be a good listener, it will not only make you a better solution provider, it will also make you understand the people you are leading, and in no time, you will become the people’s man.

BECOME AN INTIMATE FRIEND
Youths really do not appreciate leadership as much as they appreciate friendship. If you come as their leader, they are likely to keep you at a distance. To get the best cooperation from them, become their friends.

Do not just lord it over them. Irrespective of your age, come to their level. They will not initiate friendship with you, at least most of them will not. So, be the first to get close to them. Genuinely become their friends and you will discover that most of the things they naturally will hide from the other leaders, you will be privy to.

However, be careful. Do not segregate. Do not be a friend to those whose parents are rich and neglect those who seem to be less privileged. You will be courting trouble. Be a friend to all, both the straight and the wayward. Although you should not approve of his wayward lifestyle by doing anything to encourage it, but you should still be his friend anyway. Use every opportunity you have to let him know that what he is doing is bad, but still love him anyway. Remember, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us!

BE INTERESTED IN THEIR ACADEMICS
Another way in which to show that you care is to show genuine interest in their academics. Encourage them to make their academics a priority. Since most of them are likely still going to be students, you ask how each of them (or as many of them as you can) are faring academically, what challenges they are facing with other students, what challenges they are facing with problematic lecturers, what courses are causing them restless nights.

Where you can help out by your influence and position, do so wholeheartedly. Where you cannot, contact those who you know can help them on your behalf. Where the whole thing is beyond your ability, words of advice, counsel and consolation will go a long way.

Their performance in their academics must bother you as much as it bothers them. The only thing they still cherish most in their lives, today, is their academics. This is where they intend to build a future. If they falter academically and the school authority kicks them out, they become automatically discouraged with life and lose out on their destiny.

Show genuine concern in their academics, and they will be overly grateful to you. Until they know you are on their side, they probably will not come to your side.

INSPIRE THEM IN THEIR SECULAR PURSUIT
They all may not necessarily be students. Some may already be in the labor market. Do your best to inspire them in their secular pursuits. Encourage them from time to time. Ask about their

relationship with their bosses, seniors, colleagues and juniors in the office.
Ask how they are coping with the pressures of work. Challenge them to be better employees and portray themselves as good examples of the believer.

If you have any materials that could help them to improve at work, such as management books, training cds, seminar handouts, loan them with a promise from them to return at a specified time so that it will be available for others to use as well.

Lastly, find out if they are satisfied with the job or if they are there temporarily. If they really are not satisfied with it, join them in prayers, believing God for a better one. If the person in question is credible, you can make recommendation to others who can help him/her. Doing this will show that you are really interested in their progress.

CARE ABOUT THEIR PURPOSE IN LIFE
Beyond whatever job each of them is engage with presently, and beyond the academics of those who are in school, be interested in their purpose in life.

What do they want to become in the near future and on the long run? What do they want out of life? After all said and done, what do they want to be remembered for in life?

Some of them have not started thinking about this. It will not even be wrong to say some of them do not even know what their purpose in life is. Help them to discover it. Open their eyes to the great untapped resources which they have inside them, and help them tap into these. They will always remember you.

MEDIATE BETWEEN THEIR PARENTS AND THEM
A good youth leader is a mediator between the youths and their parents.

It is not uncommon for youths to have grudges against their parents. This happens from time to time. If you have worked your way into their heart, as their friends, they most likely will let you know anytime there is a riff between their parents and them. They will not tell a leader this, they will only tell a friend.

If you are opportune to be told, do not just let it end there. Correct them if you have to. Let them know that what they have done is bad. Insist they apologize to their parents. If you know their parents, you can apologize on their behalf. If you don’t, you can ask for their number and call them to tender an apology. This will make the parents trust you the more, knowing fully well that their youths are in safe hands.

Peradventure the parents are wrong, you will still have to device a way of talking to them maturely, and to let them know that things have changed from the way they were in their generation.

Once you begin to meddle into family matters like this, it shows your influence is crossing generational borders. The parents will no longer see you as their son’s or daughter’s leader, but a family friend whom they can confide in. And once in a while, they may call you to come speak with your ‘boy’ or your ‘girl’. This is a great honor to any leader who understands it.

CELEBRATE WITH THEM
Remember the special days in their lives and celebrate with them on such days. Days like their birthdays should constantly be remembered. If you find it difficult remembering these days, mark them on your office calendar or cell phone reminder.

If you can, get them a gift on their birthday. No matter how small, it will be appreciated. If you cannot get a gift, at least, make a call or send a text to wish them a great day. Matriculation day, Convocation day, First day at work, Wedding day and the like are all very special days in the lives of your youths. Make these really special for them by adding some colors to them.

MEET THEIR NEEDS
The youth ministry is capital intensive. As a youth leader, be prepared to spend money.

Youths have many needs. As discussed earlier, one major need is finance. You must be ready to meet their needs as the Lord blesses you.

Their financial problem should become your financial problem. Those who have challenges at home and who now see you as a friend, will confide in you. They necessarily may not expect that you should foot all of their bills. They just want to confide in a friend.

However, if you can afford it, as much as the Lord blesses you, meet their needs. If you do, the good Lord will reward your labor of love, and the youths will not forget that you were right by them when they needed a pillar to lean on

BE INTERESTED IN THEIR LOVE LIFE
Show genuine interest in their love life and their sexual relationships. In this age where most students do not graduate from school before age twenty two, most of them get hooked up in a serious relationship before leaving school.

The likelihood is that some of your followers are engaged, whether you believe it or not. Most of them may still be considered too young to go into such relationships, but peer pressure could make a youth do what he really does not want to do.

Whether or not you are engaged, it becomes important that you are genuinely interested in the relationships of those who are honorably engaged in the group you lead! Ask how they are coping, and if needed, give a word of advice every now and then. The reason is simple: if a youth, male or female is emotionally imbalanced, it will affect every other area of his life, including his participation in your youth group. So, be interested in their affairs.

You will also need to show your interest in the love life of those who are not yet engaged, although you will need to be cautious and diplomatic about this if you are not yet engaged yourself, else you will be sending out a wrong signal.

If any one of them is old enough to be in a relationship and is not yet in any, show you care by asking why he is not yet in a relationship. Advise him relatively, but please be matured about it; respect their opinions.

If they are not yet old enough to be in a relationship, encourage them to focus more on their academics in order to build a strong foundation for the future. Teach the females the tricks which male students are likely to use to get them hooked in unprofitable relationships and how to avoid them. Teach the males to live sexually chaste lives in a sexually corrupt world.

SCHEDULE INTERESTING ACTIVITIES
From time to time, schedule interesting activities. The activities could either be spiritual or social, with a spiritual undertone.

You can organize games or sports competition, among them, musical concerts, film shows, love feasts, guest days, excursions, field trips, visitation to the beach, museum, or zoo.

These programs always appeal to the hearts of young people and will keep their interest.

CHOOSE COMPETENT WORKFORCE
It is your responsibility to choose who you want to work with. Take your time to creatively choose a competent workforce.

Screen your followers and pick the best that you can conveniently work with and who will not pose any threat to your leadership. This factor is discussed in detail in a later chapter because of its importance.

ADDITIONAL RESPONSIBILITIES FOR A YOUTH PASTOR/MINISTER

DISCOVER THEIR DESTINY IN CHRIST

If you pastor a youth church, it will be your responsibility to help each youth discover his destiny in Christ. Let them not remain babes for too long; help them to grow spiritually. Help them to discover their spiritual gifts, their talents, and their callings.

No one prays to have a child with stunted growth. If you lead them for a whole year, and there is no visible spiritual growth in their lives, it means you have not been doing what you should do. Each year must welcome a new level of spiritual progress in the lives of your youth. This is when you can call yourself a youth minister.

ENCOURAGE THEM TO PRACTISE THEIR FAITH
It is also your primary responsibility to encourage them to practice their faith, especially when they are outside the church environment or in the midst of their friends.

This is one thing that is so difficult for youths to do. They want to live like the world. Your constant encouragement will make it easy for them to live to please the Lord anywhere they find themselves.

BE CREATIVE IN YOUR DELIVERY
To break through the ear - bursting noise of this age’s social networking and peer pressure with a scripturally balanced life-giving message will require some commitment on the youth pastor’s part. You will need to be creative in your delivery of the word.

Youths are exciting in nature and are not used to boring moments. Adults may endure a cold sermon because of their maturity, but youths want it lively. So bring in illustrations that will make your messages more relevant to their generation.

Present the word in a manner they can relate with. Apply each of your messages to their daily lives, teaching them how to apply Christian principles to their everyday experiences and to make a difference in the world. Do not just preach doctrines or controversial issues. Your messages must provide for them a visible and workable alternative to the world’s suggestions. If not, you will lose them to the world.

The world today is constantly bombarding the mind of today’s youths with the message that runs counterclockwise to the teachings of the scriptures. Messages of immorality, violence and reckless living are preached to them with all zeal. In order for us not to lose them to the devil’s altar call, your messages must have an answer to the silent questions of their heart.

PRAY WITH THEM
As a youth minister, it is also required of you to pray with them from time to time, especially during their moments of trials, temptations, persecutions, examinations and difficulties.

Pray with them whenever they are around you and let them feel the impact of your prayers even when they are far away from you. Let them be able to boast confidently, even when they are not around you: “I know my pastor is praying for me”.

Beside praying with them, encourage them to also pray with one another. This will help to divert their attention away from focusing on their own needs alone, to finding a means of helping others.

ORGANISE OUTREACH PROGRAMS
Your programs should not be targeted at building the members of the youth church alone. You should organize programs that will reach out to the youth in the neighboring communities of your local church.

This will help them to partake in the greatest assignment Jesus Christ gave to the church – The Great Commission. Such outreach programs done from time to time will result in an explosion of the church, in no time.

LIVE A MODEL LIFE
As much as you preach balanced scripture, also live a balanced life. Do what you preach. Do not live a double standard life. Be an exemplary leader.
Try as much as possible to live the life you preach so that you will not lose your credibility with your followers.

ENCOURAGE ADULT VOLUNTEERS
You will need volunteers to work with you. It is your duty as a youth pastor to encourage adult volunteers from the main church to join you in modeling the youths.

This will alleviate the pressure of the work on you, both financially and other wise. In addition to the reduction in pressure, the youths will also have a number of role models and mentors beside you, there by giving them a wide range of exposures.

SPECIAL NEWSLETTER
Organize for them a special take home newsletter. This newsletter will contain things like upcoming events of the church, special celebrants list, relevant articles to build their faith, games and puzzles.

This will keep them busy all through the week.

CONCLUSION
These and many more will facilitate a healthy relationship between you and them, and a healthy relationship with them will result in more support from them. If need be, make phone calls, write unsolicited notes of encouragement, visit them once in a while send e-mails and text messages. The texts & mails do not always have to be personalized for each of them in case there is no such time (if there is enough time there is no harm in that, it’s the best). Do not be too busy replying mails, answering calls and scheduling appointments such that you will not have time to run with your goals and aspirations. Balance it up.
