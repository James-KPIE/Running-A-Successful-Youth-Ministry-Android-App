Chapter Five

QUALITIES OF A GOOD LEADER

Described in this chapter are those characteristics that are commonly found in a good leader. If you are a good leader, you will discover that most of them are already operating in your life. However, if you find out that none of them is obvious in your life, then you may need to change your ways and strive to become a better leader than you are.

HUMILITY
In your relationship with your youths, show some level of humility. Being their leader today is just a privilege. It is not by your expertise, experience or professionalism. You are the leader today, any of them can be the leader tomorrow. So, exhibit qualitative character traits.

Do not look down on anyone in your team. Most leaders are often proud, rude, showy and pushy simply because they feel they have all the good ideas and are as such indispensable. Do not allow power get into your head. What you have is just an opportunity to serve. You really may not have gotten to the leadership position because you are the most qualified for the post. Very few of those who are really qualified for it are lucky enough to get the opportunity to serve among youths. This is why you should not abuse the privilege given to you. It is a rare one.

Do not disappoint whoever it is that has given you the opportunity to lead. Do not let your followers regret having you as a leader. It is a common saying that power corrupts, but do not let this saying be confirmed during your stay in office.

It is a deadly perception to make the costly assumption that the whole organization revolves around you. The organization will function quite well with or without you. There were other leaders in the past who occupied the same post you are occupying today; many of them thought that no one could fit adequately in their place when they were about to leave, but that was proved wrong. You will not be the last person to man the sit. Someone else will still come after you. So, you will be doing yourself a lot of good if you constantly remind yourself that the office is a temporary one, it is not a family title nor is it a life time post.

It is possible that you unconsciously begin to think that you are perfect. Some leaders are of the opinion that things happening around them happen because of what they do, what they know, and who they are. They think they are the best things that ever happened to their group, and so, are indispensable.

Watch it; whatever happens, do not let it go into your head; it is not you, it is God. You are human and can still make mistakes. That you have had a number of perfect ideas that worked out well does not mean all your ideas will be perfect.

Pride is an ever present enemy of leaders. It has been said that the strongest chains break at the weakest point. Pride is that point for many leaders. All leaders constantly face an uncontrollable urge to exhibit self-promotion. Beware, anytime you begin to get so crazy and want to singularly get all the credit for the progress made so far, Pride is about to set in.

Do not put yourself on a pedestal too high. Remember the poem you learnt in primary school: He that is down needs fear no fall. Never accord to yourself more respect or honor than you deserve. Never give to yourself more credit than you are supposed to, neither allow others force you to. Trim yourself and cut yourself to size from time to time.

RESPECTS ALL FOLLOWERS
Respect your followers. You are a leader, not a boss. The fact that you have a measure of influence over them does not make them your servants. Everybody deserves some level of respect.

Value your followers. Recognize each individual’s difference and respect it. Your leadership does not give you the liberty to address them carelessly. Watch your words. Do not be rude or offensive. Handle everyone with care. Apologize to anyone if you need to. You cannot correct disobedience in others if you do what is wrong and refuse to apologize.

Remember, respect is reciprocal. Respect yourself and you will be respected; respect others, and you will be respected the more. Others will treat you the way you relate to them. If you respect them, they will respect you.

Another advantage of showing respect to them is that when you show respect to your followers, it builds their self-esteem and self-confidence, and the greater their self-esteem and confidence, the better they appreciate you, their leader, and the more committed they will be to your dream and your regime.

There are seven major things you need to respect about your followers.
1. Respect their opinions and ideas.
2. Respect their time.
3. Respect them as humans.
4. Respect their commitment to you.
5. Respect their contribution to your dream.
6. Respect their relationships.
7. Respect their achievements.

MAN OF INTEGRITY
Leadership among youths is either by election or by appointment. Whichever category you fall into, you will need to maintain a life of integrity. Integrity is defined as moral uprightness. Have a high standard of morality.

As a leader, your most prized possession is your integrity. You must not lose it. It is most likely that you are still quite young and do not know the possibilities that the future holds for you. So, guard your integrity.

Whatever impression these ones you lead have of you today will last a long time. So, if you aspire that your leadership skills be extended beyond the youths to the society at large, you will need to build for yourself a great name and protect your integrity today for the sake of the future.

Even if you are neither elected nor appointed, this fact does not mean you are not accountable and answerable to anybody. You are responsible to God. Your followers may be mere youths today; tomorrow, some of them may become a voice in the country or may be in the position to determine the destinies of men to be elected or appointed into positions of authority. They will, likely, remember you then, either for good or for bad.

Integrity needs to be at the very core of your being. It should form the basic foundation of your leadership. If your leadership responsibility is directly under another leader’s supervision; be honest in your dealings with him. Do not try to blackmail him or kick him out of office in order to take over power from him. Putting out the candles of others will not make yours shine brighter. Do not aspire to outdo him by unjust methods. If you are destined to be there, at God’s own time he will lift you higher in his own way and that will be more honorable.
Make a solemn determination to live a straightforward and truthful life, whether someone is looking or not. Men of integrity will do in the public what they would do in the secret and vice versa.

Your integrity will gain for you more respect than you can ever purchase with eye service. Power, position, authority, may make your followers obey you (either willfully or forcefully) but they will not grant you respect. Respect is earned, and its price is integrity.

Integrity is more important than success. Most times, your integrity guarantees your success. If what you are after is success by all means, you may trade your integrity for it. However, if you try to guard your integrity jealously, success, popularity and fame will come looking for you someday.

Ethical and moral standards must be maintained. You will, of surety, face opposition in its multifaceted forms, but always stand for the truth. Men of integrity always stand for what they know to be right, even in the face of great antagonism, and most times, at an unbearable personal cost.

You cannot have peace if you sacrifice truth. Always say the truth, come what may; it will someday come back to vindicate you.

FINANCIAL ACCOUNTABILITY
Be accountable, especially when it comes to the flow of funds. Financial accountability is a must. Monetary issues must be handled with all transparency.
As a leader, your testimony as regards finances must not be questionable. Your life must be open enough for all men, especially your followers, to see. Live above suspicion and reproach as much as you can.

Do not be selfish. Think of others primarily. Your first priorities must be for the benefit of your followers and not yours. Nothing stirs up anger and rebellion among youths more than discovering that their leader is only interested in using his privileged position to selfishly acquire wealth, fame and connections for himself alone without performing that for which he is elected.

Actually, there is nothing wrong in using your position to justly accumulate wealth, acquire fame, and contact needed connections, but this should not be your priority. It should come naturally as a reward of your meritorious service to humanity. If it becomes your primary objective, you are sure to miss the mark. Face your job squarely, and every other thing will come as dividends of your selfless effort.

Put service to others first. Your desire must be to move the people forward. If your focus in office is mainly to acquire wealth before your tenure is over, you will bend the rules for money. The focus should be to be a blessing, then the money will freely flow in. The magnitude of the problem you solve during your administration will determine the reward that will come to you.

Do not set out with an intention to be rich. Set out with an intention to serve others in every way you can. If wealth is your all-consuming goal, you will soon be consumed by the fiery furnace it will create. Building your organization will benefit you much more than building your bank account.

Beware of money. Any leadership devoid of financial integrity is poor leadership. Make sure every fund is used for what it is budgeted for. If it has to be diverted to other means, make sure it is with the consent of at least a major part of the decision makers, if not all.
Do not indulge in any act that will bring disgrace to your office. Purify yourself from everything that contaminates your exalted office. Do not succumb to that greedy and selfish spirit that exists among most leaders out there. Place the need of your organization ahead of your personal aspirations. Ask yourself this question: “am I in this leadership position to bless and affect lives or to bless and affect my own pocket alone?”

Maintain a disciplined lifestyle. So many youths are of the opinion that leadership is about embezzlement of funds. They have it written somewhere in their brain that no youth vie for a leadership position simply for the sake of humanitarian service but for selfish ambitions.

Once these ones begin to see you live beyond the normal average life you used to live before you get to the position of authority, they conclude you are already emptying their treasuries to better your lot. So, be careful the way you live. Watch what you spend money on. Watch what you wear. Watch what you eat. Be contented with yourself and be contented with the funds available to you for personal use.

Even if you make genuinely clean money while in the office (this is possible once in a while but it is not always the case; a higher percentage of money gotten in office is not clean money), rather than spend such money to buy the latest electronic gadgets, wears, handset, or cars, it makes more sense to invest such for the future.

This will divert the attention of the youths from you, thereby saving you unnecessary hassles. Once you are out of the office, you can enjoy your hard earned money with no one closely monitoring your life. Apply this wisdom, it is profitable.

Financial accountability will save you from being irresponsible. It will also save you from unconsciously creating imbalances.

SEXUAL PURITY
Another character of a good leader is sexual purity. The ability to control your sexual urge will go a long way in determining what kind of leader you are.

Some leaders are loose when they are around members of the opposite sex. Their moral level, when it comes to sexual dealings, is ridiculous. As a leader of the youths, you must know how to present yourself as a man of sexual integrity.
If you are already engaged, or married, be faithful to your spouse. Do not have extramarital affairs. Let what you have be enough for you.

If you are still single, enjoy your singleness. Do not use your privileged position as a way of displaying your sexual prowess. Live a chaste life. If you must be engaged, let the process of making your choice be clean and void of stories of sexual harassment. Be an exemplary leader.

Recognize the need for personal discipline in your sexual life.

SELFLESS SERVICE
Youth leadership is all about service. Most often, the authority to lead comes naturally to those who selflessly offer genuine service to the people. This is because the very heart of leadership is service.

Selfishness and self-centeredness are the undoing of most leaders. Your responsibility, being a leader, is to serve others. Most people in leadership have problems with this. The last thing they want to do is serve others. They will rather be lord of lords. The assumption is that since the leader is at the top of the leadership rank, others below should, of necessity, serve him. However, things do not work this way.

To be a good leader, you must live a selfless life. Leading itself is spelt S-E-R-V-I-C-E. A leader who cannot serve well cannot lead well. Serving others is leadership in action. You must be ready to serve the two classes of people in your followership: the appreciative and the unappreciative.

Good Leaders give more than words and knowledge to others; they give themselves for the benefit of others. They are generous with their time and energy. They serve others by listening, supporting, encouraging, affirming, confronting, helping, loving, empathizing with others, and being there when they are needed.

Your desire must always be to serve and not to be served. You are not a director, you are a leader. A director sits down, folds his hands and directs people on what to do. A leader gets to the venue of the projects, and is the first to do what he expects others to do.

This was Jesus’ style of leadership; the leader of all is the servant of all.

RESOURCE MANAGER
One prominent challenge you will face is scarce resources. Most of the things you will want to do may not really get done the best way you desired to. A number of factors will be responsible for this:

1. You do not have all the human resources you need
2. You do not have all the financial resources you need
3. You do not have all the material resources you need
4. You do not even have full control over the available resources.

With all these limitations, you will still need to make up your mind to do your best to complete every project you undertake under whatever circumstance you find yourself in, in other to save your face and to maintain your integrity. Your success rate is measured by what you accomplished and not just what you started.

It is left to you to know how to manage these scarce resources for the progress of your projects. Look beyond what you do not have to what you have. To keep lamenting over what you do not have is to waste what you have.

Man naturally underrates what he has and overrates what he does not have. Look inward. With proper planning and judicious management of available resources, you can still perform wonders. Give the best in whatever circumstance you find yourself, with the resources available to you at the time.

COURAGEOUS IN HIS ACTIONS
A leader needs courage. To achieve your set goals, you need a strong level of confidence in yourself and in your ability as a leader. No one will believe in you if you do not believe in yourself. No one wants to follow a coward.

Youths want leaders they can look up to; someone who can be their hero and lead them all the way through. If you do not portray yourself as one you will soon have a divided house. If you are not bold enough, you will lose the youths to a follower who can take the lead boldly. They will revolt against you and
choose their own leader, and this does not take youths time to do. Although you may still be lucky to retain the title, someone else will be carrying out the functions attached to the title. You will only be a figurehead.

Whatever time it will take you, build up courage in yourself. Exercise courage in all circumstances, whether the chips are up or down. Even when doubts prevail and hope for the future erodes in your heart, let courage show on your face whenever you appear in public.

HONEST IN HIS DEALINGS
Honesty, they say, is the best policy. Be honest; keep your activities above reproach, both before God and men. Stand for the truth, even in the face of opposition. Do not allow yourself to be pushed around. Maintain your stand.

Honesty gives you credibility. It builds trust and faith in your followers. Tell them the truth the way it is. Never be found to make untrue statements for whatever cause, just or unjust. If you want the youths to accept you as a godly role model, then you must live a godly life. Do not amplify facts or figures. Do not exaggerate issues, augment statistics or cook up testimonies to back up your words. Say it the way it is.

Do not pretend to be a know-it-all leader. Tell them the little you know and let them know you will get back to them after consulting with higher authorities. If asked a question about which you have no answer, let them know. This will neither reduce you nor make them look down on you.

You do not have to say anything if you do not have to. It will make them respect you the more as a sincere leader, and they are likely to open up to you the more about more critical issues in their personal lives which they have kept secret from their parents.

Honesty is the key to successful leadership. Operate in openness, fairness and uprightness in all of your dealings. It will foster unity between you and your followers.

GENEROUS WITH APPRECIATION
Be generous with appreciation; a good leader is quick to praise. Appreciate the commitment of others to your goals. Every worthwhile and constructive contribution to the success of your goal, no matter how minute, must be credited. Every accomplishment, big or small, must be recognized. This will keep the youths interested in you, and in your leadership goals.

God will not connect you to new people if you are messing up with the ones around you. Always express appreciation for work done. This can be by writing short appreciation notes, making phone calls, text messages and e-mails. These will build their morale the more and spur them to better action when next their services are required.

Good words satisfy. Sweet words soothe. Word of praise nourishes. People cooperate more when they are appreciated than when they are reproached. Great comments stir up greater feats. Commendations give them inner confidence: it encourages the people to achieve more. Do not take them for granted.

Beyond their commitment to you, praise every noticeable personal improvement in the lives of your followers and encourage others to imitate them.

HE IS PATIENT
It takes time to get the full cooperation of youths. Contrary to what you want, they will not all give you their support overnight. You will have to earn it.

You must be patient with them while they decide whether to cooperate with you for the success of your ideas or to stay on their own. Do not be forceful. Do not be pushy. Do not be too hasty. Do not pressure them to respond to you.

Give them time to get used to you and your style of operation. It is only then that their cooperation will come wholeheartedly, and then long lasting. If you force them into supporting you before it comes from their hearts, the best you will get from them is partial obedience and eye service, and you will not really enjoy them as much as you should.

So, a good leader will need patience to handle them, not just to get their cooperation at the beginning of his administration alone, but to enjoy a good team work with them all through the period of his tenure.

HE IS TOLERANT
In addition to your patience, you will also need to be tolerant. There are things you may not like to accommodate but you will have to learn to tolerate them for the sake of peace. Tolerance is the ability to accept or forbear what one may not naturally permit.

Since you are leading the youths, you will definitely need to tolerate some of their excesses, as long as it does not bring any major setback to the general wellbeing and the unity of the group, and it does not have a genuine conflict with the scriptures.

There will be times when you will feel like screaming at some of them and shouting them down. Please be careful to restrain yourself, at least in the public. Put your emotions under check else it will interfere with your work.

Do not be quick to condemn or criticize. Do not show personal hatred for anyone because of his excesses. Do not take things too personal. Be reasonable. Be willing to take insults from others without a feeling of hostility. In fact, ignore insult; because you will have more than you can handle! A whole chapter in this book is dedicated to how to handle criticism and insult. This is to show you how important it is. Be tolerable.

HE IS FORGIVING
People will often hurt you, so be prepared to forgive them in advance. Forgive every critic. Forgive every workforce. Forgive every executive. Forgive every youth. Forgive your protégés. Forgive your mentors if need be.

The sign of maturity in a leader is not in how many times he has been hurt but how fast he recovers from each of the hurts. Forgive everyone who offends you each time they do, whether they deserve it or not. Forgive as often as possible. A good leader is always quick to forgive and he is never slow to ask for forgiveness where applicable.

HE IS APPROACHABLE
Be conscious of the things happening around you. Let every member of your group, as much as possible, have access to you. Be friendly and welcoming. Do not build fences around yourself. The door that is created to lock others out also shuts you in.

Be charming. Always wear a good look with a warm smile. It will bring people closer to you. Learn to smile a lot, you are not a military president. A good leader is more of the image of Jesus of Nazareth who goes about doing good, than that of the Lion of the tribe of Judah. Your position as a leader, coupled with the authority and power available to you may create in you a tendency to want to intimidate others unconsciously. Guard against it.

Make yourself reachable. Do not become a god to your people. Be available when they need you. Put measures in place that will ensure that your followers who seriously need your attention get it before it is too late (as much as your time will allow you).

HE KEEPS PROMISES
Do not make promises you cannot keep. Be a man of your word. The thin line between trust and distrust is in how much of your promises you keep.

The more of your promises you keep, the more the trust your followers will have in you, and the more their confidence in your ability.

When you constantly disappoint them, saying things you cannot do, irrespective of the reasons you may have for failing them, they will not find reasons to want to trust you again. This will be disastrous to your leadership.

A good leader always promises what he can deliver, but tries to outdo his own promises so that at the end of the day, he has done much more than his followers are expecting from him. This gains him more respect and love from his people.

HE RAISES OTHER LEADERS
There is little to what you can, single-handedly, do alone. Rather than focus only on his ability, a good leader concentrates on developing other leaders who will make a positive difference.

You will always have more to do than you will have time to do them. Therefore, your impact only becomes a lasting one if you can develop more genuine leaders from your followers who also will develop other leaders.

Jesus had only twelve disciples. Today, the world is filled with millions of Christian leaders who still trace their roots down to Him. He only deposited Himself into twelve people and the twelve passed the legacy on to numerous others, and that way, He has been able to engrave the continuity of His leadership and His doctrine on the rock of eternity.
