Chapter Two

RESPONSIBILITIES OF A YOUTH LEADER (1)

Once you are appointed a youth minister or a student leader, your first action should be to know what is required of you. Leadership is responsibility. It is not enough to carry titles and just sit in offices. You need to know what your duties entail. This is what is called job description, in some quarters.

Each title in any standard establishment has its own job description. The essence is to help one to know precisely what duties are assigned to him, what other responsibilities are expected of him in that office and to avoid clash of duties and responsibilities with other personnel. Your leadership office also has some job specifications attached to it which may not coincide with any other person’s. Neglect of such leadership functions will portray you as a leader who is not worth his title.

The assessment of your success as a leader will be based on the impact you make on the lives of your followers. Whether you leave them better or worse than when you met them will be the question. If you perform your responsibilities well as a leader, your followers will be the better for it; if you do not, it will also tell on them.

What improvement did you bring into their lives as individuals and into the assembly in general? What fresh opportunities did you introduce them to? If none, they may be quiet about it during your regime; but trust youths: when your tenure is over, you will hear the true assessment of your regime.

Every leader should be concerned about this main question - What will people say about me at the end of my regime? Whatever they will say at the end of your tenure, good or bad, will depend largely on how much you have impacted their lives during the course of your leadership.

Leadership is more of impact on a group of followers and not a force that drives the people. What influence do you have over your people? Have you influenced their lives positively since you became their leader? This is one of the factors that will determine your acceptability.

Discussed below are three of your major responsibilities to your followers -

(1) DISCOVER THEIR POTENTIALS
Do your best to discover the potentials in each of your followers. Everyone may not be gifted in all things but everyone is gifted in, at least one thing. If you take your time to investigate, you will discover that, most often than not, all the human resources you need to succeed as a leader are among your followers. You need no foreign expert’s help, at least at the initial stage. You need no mercenary from other places to aid you. Every human ability you need to take the group forward is in the lives of the people.

Everyone has something to offer. Although, each of them is a separate individual with unique needs, concerns and desires. Beyond these challenges, every one of your followers has great talents, wonderful skills and enormous resources that could move the organization forward during your regime. Everyone has his own weaknesses. Rather than keep fighting them for their weaknesses, take time to discover their potential strength and encourage them to build on it.

All you need, you have. All you have, you need. There is a wealth of potentials in the people you are called to lead. It may not look like it at this initial stage. Most of them have not and may never discover it until someone helps them to; and you are in the best position to do just that!

Most of the people you are called to lead are in a blind man’s position. With all of the abundant endowments of God in their lives, they still cannot see their value and relevance to their immediate society. They need someone to help them discover their latent abilities, hidden potentials, and unexplored gifts. This is one reason why God placed you as a leader over them; to help them discover their gifts and abilities.

Since most of the youths you lead will likely be students, you may want to assume that their lecturers will do this. It has been discovered overtime that lecturers do not have the time for personal issues like this; they are more concerned with lecturing, setting exam questions and marking answer scripts than having time for anything private with students, most of whom they have no personal relationship with.

Many of their friends who should help them with this are not interested in this either; their interest is more in their social life, companionship and friendship. Anything outside this is not important enough to be involved in.

Their parents are more occupied with thoughts of where to get money for the sponsorship of their education and catering for their general welfare and total needs, that such issues have become non-essentials to them.

Whoever God uses as the instrument for this discovery will be adored by them all of their lives. Be that person. Discover one special thing that each person can do.

If you lead quite a large group that you cannot personally have a direct contact with everyone, try to influence the few you meet on daily basis. Before you know it, one at a time, you would have influenced almost all. Your influence on the ones you meet per day will have a rippling effect on the others.

Discovering these potentials will become easy for you if you always look at your followers with the eyes of the future. Appreciate much more what they can become tomorrow than what they simply are today. This is the way God operates. He focuses more on potentials that everyone else overlooks. Be like God; discover something useful in everyone around you.

Watch out for people in whose lives you can make personal investments. Determine to make the necessary investments to make them better. Help everyone see the good in themselves.

Lastly, recognize the latent leadership abilities in your followers. Some of them have leadership skills that are not yet discovered. Be alert to it and make use of it for the advancement of the group.

This is the difference between leaders who raise leaders and those who raise followers; the former focus on their strength while the latter focus on their weaknesses.

(2) DEVELOP THE POTENTIALS
The second major duty you have as a leader is to help every one of your followers release the potentials you have discovered in them. This, in another word could be called mentoring; mentor your followers.

Everyman is created to help others bring out the best in them. This is more paramount for a leader. Every leader must help his followers to become the best they are capable of becoming.

Help them to develop what you have discovered about them. Helping others up the ladder keeps you close to the top of the ladder. Teach them and train them to maturity. To succeed more in your office as their leader, nurture to greatness each follower’s best talents and abilities; they will be grateful to you.

Build up your work force with all that you have. This may not be too easy to do. It may cost you some money. It may cost you some part of your personal time. To make it worse, some may not even be appreciative of this effort. Some may think that you are just being too demanding by interfering in their personal issues.

Whatever your followers think about your actions today, time will tell who is right. The few who are willing to follow your counsel and guidelines today will prove to the others tomorrow that you know what you are doing. Those who accuse you today will come to agree with you in the nearest future that you were right and that they were wrong.

(3) EXPLORE THE POTENTIALS
After you have discovered and developed the potentials in each of your followers, use the developed potential for the good of
the group. So many leaders do not do this. These leaders allow developed talents to lie wasted until someone from outside the group discovers the already developed talents, hijacks the individuals, and begins to explore these talents. This is laxity on the part of these leaders.

Most times, these leaders blame it on the hijacker. They feel the hijacker did not allow the young talents to grow into maturity before harvesting the fruits of someone else’s labor. They wanted the youths in question to develop into adulthood before committing serious things into his hands. It is not the hijacker’s fault. He saw good seeds fallen carelessly on the rock, and decided to move them to fertile grounds, rather than allow the sun to do injustice to it. Whatever their excuse, they have lost an investment, and someone else is benefitting from it.

A good leader will be willing to help every follower to correctly explore their potent talents and latent potentials, investing these in the progress of the group. If you do not do this last one, then all tasks which you have done in their lives prior to this time will be wasted effort to you; but not to them, because someone else (who did not know when you were doing all the investments in them) will benefit from your labor.

There are two types of leaders; those who develop leaders and those who develop followers. The difference between these two is not just what they deposit in their disciples, but how much of what is deposited is explored before they are left to try it out on their own. Almost all leaders deposit something, no matter how little, in their followers. Not all are willing to, patiently, wait to see their followers try their hands on what they have consciously or unconsciously learnt from them.

In order for you to be a good youth leader, you must transform the ordinary youths around you to influential youths by exploring their internal configurations. Do not just abandon them once you have discovered and helped them developed their talents. Give them trial opportunities. You will be surprised at what they will deliver.

When you make the best use of your own people, you will be amazed at the super human feats you will accomplish through them. Train them up, then place them where their potentials can produce maximal results. The youths you trained today will carry on the baton tomorrow. Train them to maturity and watch them display. Give them opportunities to manifest. The major advantage of this is that it empowers them enough to carry on your dreams and projects with or without you.

After developing them to a stage, you would have reproduced yourself in them and spread your influence all over them, thereby empowering them to carry on the vision even long after your tenure. This way, your impact will last longer on them. You would have produced enough successors by training enough people who will fill the vacuum and carry on your legacies after you are long gone.

This is the only way to be truly successful as a leader; to help your followers succeed. Both parties end up with success stories. These are your three major responsibilities as a youth leader.

OTHER RESPONSIBILITIES

Beside these three general responsibilities of all leaders stated in this chapter, there are other specific, more targeted responsibilities. These will be discussed in the next chapter. The assessment of your leadership will be based on how well you are able to fulfill all of these responsibilities put together. If you are new to the system and you do not know what some of these responsibilities may be, feel free to ask the following people:

1.) YOUR PREDECESSORS
These ones have occupied this same position before you. Some of them have done wonderfully well. Others have done poorly. Hearing from these people will help you know what to do and what not to do. You will be able to build on the successes of those who succeeded and avoid the pitfalls of those who failed

2.) YOUR KINGMAKERS
These are the ones who are responsible for your appointment into leadership. They, very likely, would have given you a detailed job description. Whether they have or not, you may still need to meet some of them personally and ask them what is really expected of a fellow who occupies the seat you are occupying. Since they were responsible for your being chosen, they should be able to tell you the type of leaders they were sourcing for when they considered you as the best option.

3.) YOUR FOLLOWERS
Some of your followers may be lucky to have survived more than one or two tenures of leadership in the same group. They have seen it all; the good, the bad and the ugly. Discussing with them about what is expected of an ideal leader in this group could open up a great discussion that will be of benefit to you.

Combining the list from these three categories of people will give you a vivid description of the functions that are expected of you so that you will not be found either going beyond your boundary or performing below expectation. Time is too short to play “trial and error”. Know what you are called to do.

However, do not be psychologically caged by this list. Let your imagination and creativity run wild. Only allow the list to guide you so that you will not fall where others before you have fallen.
