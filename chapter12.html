Chapter Twelve

WHAT MOTIVATES YOUR ACTION

On a careful check, you will discover that the list you compiled in the previous chapter which consists of the projects you want to accomplish during your administration is motivated by one of four factors. Every leader is motivated by these factors. Whichever of them motivates your actions will determine how successful you will be.

EGO MOTIVATED

So many leaders do things in order to satisfy their ego. They consult nobody. They get ideas, advice, and counsel from nobody. They just take their decisions based on whatever little information is available to them and they expect others to follow blindly, whether they agree with their decisions or not. These are power drunk leaders.

Leadership is not all about the leader; it is all about the followers. It is not about what you can do for yourself but about what you can do for the group. If you are more concerned about yourself than about the people, then you are ego motivated and will not last as the leader.

Leadership is also about service. Every good leader should be concerned about rendering effective service to his followers and not just be authoritarian. If you also desire to be a good leader, you must aspire to be a servant-leader and not a dominating leader.

A good leader, though firm, is not rigid. Rigid authoritarians, also known as bosses, show the way and compel others to go the way. No one confronts them; they are egocentric. However, firm authoritative leaders show the way, lead the way, and convince others to follow.

There are lots of differences between being authoritative and being authoritarian. To be authoritarian is to be dominating, harsh, oppressive, dictatorial and tyrannical. To be authoritative is to be directional and supervisory. As a leader, there will always be an uncontrollable urge to dominate your follower, but this should be continually put under check.

Do not lust after prominence and power; else you will be so obsessed by it that you will comfortably climb on anyone at will to get it.

MASSES MOTIVATED

A mass-motivated leader is one who does not have his own mind or rarely uses it. He dances to every tune played by the masses. He really cannot stamp his feet if he needs to, and he cannot convince his followers to do the right thing. Rather than govern, he is governed. He easily caves in to wrong doing when pressured by the crowd. He is scared of everything. He is scared of losing power; scared of losing his position; scared of losing the people; scared of losing anything or everything. He is a fear-filled ruler.

If you are a mass-motivated leader, you will need to change your ways if you must lead rightly. Learn to do the right thing; regardless of what others think. If you are in the habit of allowing your followers manipulate you, you are not the leader they need. There will always be a strong pressure on you from some of the youths to do what is popular rather than what is right. Do not compromise your stand. Use your mind.

Never embark on any project for fear of rejection or criticism, especially when it is not the right action to take. Only do what you are supposed to do. If you carry out projects purposely to please some sets of people, it means you are being manipulated by some of your followers to the detriment of the rest, and the end result may not be too pleasant if they get to know of this. Do only what you have to do.

Know how to say no when you have to. One of the most difficult things for people in authority to do is to say no to what the majority of the people want. However, if what the people want is not what they need, rather than say yes to all of their biddings, take your time to educate them and let them see the potential danger in what they desire. Then, give them a better alternative to what they want. Youths, though difficult to lead, are very reasonable followers. Once you can make them see reason, majority of your followers will be convinced of your actions. If you have been a good leader before now, and you have been able to gain their trust from the beginning of your tenure, they will trust your judgment and follow the path of your “better” alternative.

Jesus disappointed all His followers who thought they could manipulate Him because of their intimacy with Him. The two sons of Zebedee wanted the two seats on His right and left side in His kingdom, but they were disappointed. They even brought their mother to plead on their behalf but Jesus turned deaf hears to them. Peter, as close as he was to Jesus, was going to stop Him from going to Jerusalem. He also was embarrassed. There were many other instances where Jesus’ close pals thought they could control Him at will, but He never allowed Himself to be manipulated.

Learn from Jesus. Never follow the masses when they are wrong. Lead your followers to do the right thing.

DREAM MOTIVATED

A dream-motivated ruler is one who is only interested in building a name for himself and will stop at nothing to achieve this selfish goal. He is more interested in projects than in people.

He embarks on gigantic projects that are beyond the capacity of both himself and his followers, and then drives his followers crazy to get the projects accomplished, without caring a bit about how they feel. He goes from one major project to another, using the people as mere tools to get his projects accomplished and his ambitions achieved. He selfishly focuses more on his projects to the detriment of his people, and finds it easier to use people than to grow them.

This is not fair. A good leader should focus on both, but more on the people. Rather than drive your people heartlessly to build your projects, a good leader will spend more of his time and resources building the people, knowing fully well that, after they are well established, the people he has built will support him in building laudable projects. Great leaders build the people who, in turn, build the projects.

Be more interested in the people than in your dreams and projects. The people should be your first target. If the people can fall in love with you, they will buy into your projects and you will not need to force them to carry them on. They will willingly, even beyond what you expect.

If you are one of the leaders who use people only to accomplish their goals, you will make undue demands on them that will wear them out. This may result in discouragement and fatigue, and you may end up losing the people, and thereafter, the project. Irrespective of the importance of the projects, if your followers are heavily worn out and unreasonably spent as at the time of accomplishment, you are not a good leader. Their enthusiasm must be maintained from the beginning to the end of

the project. It is by such action only that you gain their unflinching allegiance to you and the remaining projects.

Never place the project ahead of the people. The purpose of your projects should be to meet people’s needs. Any project that does not meet this first criterion is a wild dream. You do not have to receive any direct benefit from all of your projects. That should not be the purpose. If that is your singular purpose, you are a selfish dream-motivated leader. If all you care about is your pocket, your benefits, your name, and what comes to you, you are not an ideal leader.

The first four letters in the word ‘leader’ is spelt LEAD. Good leaders lead their followers, and their followers are always willing to follow. If you are a leader and the majority of your followers complain about your leadership style, or revolt and rebel, not desiring to follow, then something is wrong. The problem may be that you are more of a dream motivated leader. Good leaders do not lord their dreams and projects over others.

If your intention is to make use of the youths for the sole purpose of furthering your own hidden agenda, trouble looms. Do not just use them as a means to achieve your goals. They are humans, not robots. Devote more of your time and energy to people than to projects. The projects you build will someday be forgotten, but the people you build will forever remember you.

NEED MOTIVATED

These are the leaders who build their projects around their followers’ needs.

If your leadership role cannot solve the problem of the youths you are leading, you have no business being their leader. Great leaders are more particular about their followers’ needs. Until you know the need of the people and you are ready to meet it, you cannot make progress as a leader. It is the problem you solve that determines the name you earn.

Watch out for keenly felt needs and think of innovative and creative ways to solve them. These can make your influence in leadership grow steadily and rapidly. Committed leaders need not force other people to go along with them; they natural follow, simply because they are sensitive to the paramount desires of their followers.

Once your followers discover that you are a good leader with wonderful ideas, good programs and relevant projects, they celebrate you as one of the best leaders they ever had, and will, likely, follow you sheepishly thereafter because you have won their love.

Some followers may want to take advantage of your good nature and demand for things beyond your control. It will be stupid to dance to their tune. Know your limits. You are neither God, nor a magician. You are not a miracle worker. You are simply a leader. Irrespective of the quarters from where the pressure may come from, resist the temptation to take responsibility for things you cannot control. Trying to please such people will only bring you frustration since what they are demanding for is beyond your position and your power. Unrealistic goals will always result in unnecessary disappointments.

CONCLUSION

What motivates you to do the things you want to do? Are you dream-motivated, masses-motivated, ego-motivated, or need-motivated?
